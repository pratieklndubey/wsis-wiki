# United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO)

## Overview
UNESCO (the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization) is a UN specialised agency founded on **16 November 1945**, headquartered in **Paris, France**. Its mandate is to "promote world peace and security through international cooperation in education, arts, sciences and culture." Among the UN family, UNESCO plays a central role in the WSIS framework as the lead or co-facilitating agency for four of the eleven [[wsis-action-lines]]: **C3** (Access to Information and Knowledge), **C8** (Cultural Diversity and Linguistic Diversity), **C9** (Media), and **C10** (Ethical Dimensions).

UNESCO is one of the three principal co-organisers of the annual [[wsis-forum]] alongside [[itu]] and [[undp]], and a standing member of [[ungis]]. It co-chairs the Inter-Agency Working Group on AI (IAWG-AI) with ITU, coordinating 50 UN entities on AI policy.

## Detail

### History and Structure

UNESCO was created in the aftermath of World War II, succeeding the League of Nations' International Committee on Intellectual Cooperation. Its founding constitution states: "since wars begin in the minds of men, it is in the minds of men that the defences of peace must be constructed."

**Governance:**
- **General Conference:** All member states; meets biennially; sets programme, budget, elects Executive Board
- **Executive Board:** 58 members; meets twice yearly; manages between General Conferences
- **Secretariat:** Headed by Director-General (appointed by General Conference on Executive Board's recommendation, 4-year renewable term)

### UNESCO's WSIS Action Lines

**C3: Access to Information and Knowledge**
UNESCO leads C3 with ITU, FAO, and UNIDO as co-facilitators. Since 2006, 14 facilitation meetings have been held covering: access to public information, open-source software, community access points, open access to scientific knowledge, ICT for persons with disabilities, and gender dimensions of information access. The open access to scientific information theme reflects UNESCO's longstanding advocacy for open science policies.

**C8: Cultural Diversity and Identity, Linguistic Diversity and Local Content**
UNESCO is the sole facilitator of C8, reflecting its unique mandate on culture and communication. Since 2006, 11 facilitation meetings have addressed: cultural diversity and digital identity (2009), multilingualism online (2010), indigenous peoples and ICTs in education (2012–2014), and "culture for a sustainable digital environment" (2015, 2019). The UNESCO Convention on Cultural Diversity (2005) provides the normative foundation for C8.

**C9: Media**
UNESCO is the sole facilitator of C9. Since 2006, 15 facilitation meetings have covered: media independence and pluralism, combating illegal and harmful content, diversity of media ownership, gender portrayal, training of media professionals, and the role of traditional media in the digital transition. UNESCO's World Press Freedom Day (May 3) and the Windhoek Declaration tradition ground this work.

**C10: Ethical Dimensions of the Information Society**
UNESCO leads C10 with ECOSOC (and WHO and ECPAT International as co-facilitators). Since 2006, 12 facilitation meetings have addressed: applying universal human values to the Information Society; AI ethics (increasingly prominent since 2016); prevention of abusive uses of ICTs; children's online safety. The **UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence** (2021) — the first global normative instrument on AI ethics, adopted by 193 states — is the most significant recent C10 output and a key input to [[global-digital-compact]] AI governance provisions.

### UNESCO and the WSIS Forum

UNESCO is a co-organiser of the annual [[wsis-forum]] alongside [[itu]], [[undp]], and [[unctad]]. In practice, UNESCO anchors the cultural, educational, and ethical dimensions of the Forum, hosting dedicated C8, C9, and C10 workshops and the C3 facilitation meeting each year.

### UNESCO and AI Governance

UNESCO plays a growing role in AI governance at the interface between WSIS, the GDC, and broader digital cooperation:
- Co-chairs IAWG-AI (Inter-Agency Working Group on AI) with ITU — coordinating 50 UN entities
- Developed the **UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI** (2021), the first global normative AI ethics framework
- Participates in AI for Good (ITU-led) as a co-organiser
- C10 serves as the primary WSIS Action Line for AI ethics discussions

### Key Programmes Beyond WSIS
- **World Heritage Sites:** Designation and protection of cultural and natural heritage
- **UNESCO Chairs:** 644 chairs in 126 countries linking academia to UNESCO's mandate
- **ASPNet:** 12,000+ associated schools in 182 countries
- **Biosphere Reserves:** Man and the Biosphere Programme (since 1971)
- **Memory of the World:** International Register of documentary heritage (since 1997)
- **Endangered Languages Atlas:** Documenting and protecting linguistic diversity
