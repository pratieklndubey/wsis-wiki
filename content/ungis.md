# United Nations Group on the Information Society (UNGIS)

## Overview
The United Nations Group on the Information Society (UNGIS) — formally the **United Nations Group on the Information Society for Digital Transformation** — is the primary inter-agency coordination mechanism for WSIS implementation within the UN system. It was established in **April 2006** to coordinate substantive and policy issues among UN entities implementing the outcomes of the World Summit on the Information Society.

UNGIS operates at the intersection of all major UN digital cooperation frameworks: [[wsis-action-lines]], the [[global-digital-compact]], and the Sustainable Development Goals ([[sdgs]]). With [[itu]] as its permanent secretariat, UNGIS has 32 member organisations spanning the full range of UN specialised agencies, funds, and programmes.

UNGIS is the body directly responsible for the joint WSIS-GDC implementation roadmap mandated by [[unga-res-80-173]] (December 2025), to be delivered to [[cstd]] at its 29th session in 2026.

## Key Facts
- **Established:** April 2006 (following WSIS Phase II, 2005)
- **Full name:** United Nations Group on the Information Society for Digital Transformation
- **Mandate:** Coordinate UN system implementation of WSIS outcomes; advance digital cooperation
- **Permanent Secretariat:** [[itu]]
- **Current Chair (rotating):** UNCTAD (as of 2024/2025)
- **Vice-Chairs:** [[itu]], [[undp]], [[unesco]], UNESCWA
- **Members:** 32 UN entities
- **Key product:** UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix (maps UN initiatives to GDC objectives, WSIS Action Lines, and SDGs)
- **WSIS+20 mandate:** Prepare joint WSIS-GDC implementation roadmap for CSTD 29th session (2026); establish task force on financing mechanisms (A/RES/80/173, paras. 67, 121, 122)
- **Contact:** contact@ungis.org

## Detail

### Purpose and Functions

UNGIS was created as a direct response to the WSIS Tunis Agenda's call for coherent UN system implementation of WSIS outcomes. Its primary functions are:

1. **Coordination of UN WSIS activities:** Preventing duplication and ensuring alignment across the 32 member organisations' WSIS-related work
2. **High-level policy engagement:** Convening senior UN officials on digital governance and ICT-for-development themes
3. **Digital cooperation advancement:** Coordinating UN positions on emerging digital governance issues, including AI, data governance, and platform regulation
4. **WSIS-GDC coherence:** Since the adoption of the [[global-digital-compact]] (September 2024), a core UNGIS function is mapping and coordinating the relationship between WSIS frameworks and the GDC
5. **Gender mainstreaming:** Integrating gender equality into all UN digital initiatives

### Governance Structure

UNGIS operates at two levels:
- **High-level meetings:** Senior officials from member organisations discuss strategic direction
- **Working-level meetings:** Technical staff coordinate implementation details

The Chair rotates among members; UNCTAD currently chairs. Five Vice-Chairs (ITU, UNDP, UNESCO, UNESCWA, and one rotating) provide additional leadership. The **permanent secretariat** is hosted by ITU — the only non-rotating element of UNGIS governance, reflecting ITU's central role as WSIS lead agency.

### UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix

One of UNGIS's most significant recent outputs is the **[[ungis-gdc-matrix]]** (formally: "WSIS Process and 2030 Agenda – GDC Matrix" / "WSIS Process follow-up on the Summit of the Future Outcome Documents"), which maps:
- Every GDC commitment (5 objectives, 74 paragraphs, follow-up section)
- Against the 11 [[wsis-action-lines]] (C1–C11 and C7 sub-themes)
- Against the [[global-digital-compact]] objectives
- Against the 17 [[sdgs]]
- With named existing UN mechanisms and initiatives for each link

**28 UN co-producing agencies:** ITU, UNESCO, UNDP, UNCTAD, FAO, WFP, WHO, UPU, UNEP, ITC, ILO, UNDESA, UNHCR, UNICEF, UNIDO, UNODC, WMO, UN Women, UNECA, ECLAC, ESCAP, ESCWA, UNECE, UNJSPF, UNU, WIPO, UN-Habitat, OHCHR

This matrix is the authoritative tool for demonstrating the complementarity — rather than duplication — of the WSIS and GDC frameworks, and is referenced throughout the WSIS+20 preparatory process and outcome resolution. The matrix was foundational to the [[gdc-implementation-map]] produced by the UN Working Group on Digital Technologies (WGDT).

### UN Working Group on Digital Technologies (WGDT)

The WGDT was established to coordinate the UN system's response to the [[global-digital-compact]]. It produced the [[gdc-implementation-map]] (Draft, July 2025), which is built on the UNGIS matrix. The WGDT worked under the oversight of the Executive Office of the Secretary-General and coordinated with UNGIS, representing an overlay coordination layer during the GDC implementation startup phase. As the Office for Digital and Emerging Technologies (ODET) assumes its role post-WSIS+20, the WGDT's functions are expected to transition to ODET.

### WSIS+20 Mandates (A/RES/80/173)

The [[unga-res-80-173]] (December 2025) assigned UNGIS several critical responsibilities:

- **Para. 67:** ITU (as UNGIS secretariat) to establish an **internal task force** to assess gaps in financial mechanisms for digital development in developing countries; submit concrete recommendations to [[cstd]] 30th session (2027)
- **Para. 121:** UNGIS to continue with secretariat provided by ITU; rotating annual Chair; expand membership; enhance agility for multi-stakeholder dialogue
- **Para. 122:** UNGIS to prepare a **joint WSIS-GDC implementation roadmap** for consideration by CSTD at its 29th session (2026); roadmap to "strengthen coherence between WSIS and GDC, ensuring a unified, resource-efficient approach to digital cooperation"

These mandates make UNGIS the institutional backbone of post-2025 WSIS-GDC coherence.
