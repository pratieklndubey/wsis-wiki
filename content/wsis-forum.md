# WSIS Forum

## Overview
The WSIS Forum is the world's largest annual gathering of the ICT-for-development community. Hosted by [[itu]] and co-organised with [[unesco]], [[undp]], and [[unctad]] (in collaboration with over 40 UN entities), it is held each year in Geneva and serves as the principal mechanism for ongoing multistakeholder dialogue, knowledge-sharing, and review of progress on the eleven WSIS Action Lines.

The Forum evolved from a series of WSIS follow-up meetings that began in 2006, shortly after the close of [[wsis-phase-ii-tunis-2005]]. From 2006–2008, ITU and other Action Line facilitators held annual follow-up consultations. In 2009, this cluster of meetings was formally branded the **WSIS Forum** and has grown continuously since then — from roughly 1,200 participants in 2009 to over 50,000 multistakeholder participants (onsite and virtual) in recent years.

The Forum is both a review mechanism and a forward-looking platform. Each year it produces actionable policy recommendations, showcases best practices through the WSIS Prizes, hosts ministerial roundtables, and addresses emerging technology and governance issues. As the WSIS+20 milestone approaches (UNGA review in December 2025), the WSIS Forum — branded as the **WSIS+20 High-Level Event** in 2024 and 2025 — has served as the primary stakeholder preparatory platform for the twenty-year review.

- **Host:** [[itu]] (annual host since 2009)
- **Co-organisers:** [[unesco]], [[undp]], [[unctad]] (plus 40+ UN entities)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland (primarily [[itu]] Headquarters and CICG)
- **First edition:** 2009 (preceded by WSIS follow-up meetings 2006–2008)
- **Participation (recent years):** 50,000+ onsite and virtual participants annually
- **WSIS Prizes:** Launched at the Forum in 2012; recognises outstanding WSIS Action Line projects
- **WSIS+20 High-Level Event 2024:** 27–31 May 2024; 4,000+ onsite and online; 80+ ministers
- **WSIS+20 High-Level Event 2025:** 7–11 July 2025 at Palexpo, Geneva; held alongside AI for Good Global Summit; 11,000+ onsite; 59 ministers and deputies
- **2025 Chair:** H.E. Mr Solly Malatsi, Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies of South Africa
- **Annual themes:** Reflect current emerging technology and policy challenges

## Detail

### Origins (2006–2008)

Following the close of [[wsis-phase-ii-tunis-2005]] and in line with the [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]] (which called for ongoing multi-stakeholder implementation and follow-up), ITU together with other WSIS Action Line facilitators began hosting annual follow-up meetings. These were initially a loose cluster of related events — Action Line facilitation meetings, the Commission on Science and Technology for Development (CSTD) sessions, and open consultations — that gathered under the same roof during the same week in Geneva.

### The WSIS Forum Era (2009–present)

In 2009, the cluster was formalised as the **WSIS Forum** — a single branded annual event with its own identity, structure, and communications. Key features of the Forum across its history:

- **Annual themes:** Each year's Forum has a headline theme reflecting current digital priorities: in 2009, foundational WSIS targets for 2015; in 2015, linking WSIS Action Lines to SDGs; in 2019, ICTs for SDG acceleration; in 2020–21, digital resilience through and after COVID-19; in 2022–23, AI, digital inclusion, and digital trust; in 2024–25, WSIS+20 review and future of digital cooperation.
- **WSIS Action Line Facilitation Meetings:** Each Action Line custodian agency convenes an annual facilitation meeting as part of the Forum, reviewing progress, identifying challenges, and updating roadmaps.
- **Ministerial Roundtables:** High-level political engagement with ministers discussing national digital priorities, from early editions (20+ ministers) through to recent editions (59 ministers and deputies in 2025).
- **WSIS Prizes:** Launched in 2012, the contest recognises excellence in implementing WSIS Action Lines. By 2025, over 9,000 projects have been submitted and 252 winners plus 720 champions recognised. The WSIS Prizes have become a major global visibility mechanism for ICT-for-development projects.
- **Hackathons and side events:** From 2015 onwards, hackathons organised in partnership with UNESCO, FAO, ILO, WHO, and others have addressed digital challenges in health, agriculture, learning, and hunger.
- **WSIS+20 High-Level Events:** In 2024 and 2025, the Forum was branded as WSIS+20 High-Level Events, serving as the primary preparatory platform for the UNGA's twenty-year overall review scheduled for December 2025.

### Structure and Process

The annual Forum typically runs for five days and includes:
- **High-level policy sessions:** Keynote addresses by heads of state and government, ministers, and UN leaders
- **Ministerial Roundtable:** Ministers discuss national digital priorities and WSIS future
- **Leaders TalkX:** Structured high-level dialogue sessions (introduced in recent editions)
- **Thematic workshops:** 200–500 sessions per edition covering all WSIS Action Lines and emerging topics
- **WSIS Action Line Facilitation Meetings:** Action Line custodian agencies meet to review progress and update implementation roadmaps
- **WSIS Prizes ceremony:** Winners and champions announced
- **Exhibition space:** 50+ booths from UN agencies, governments, and private sector (2025)
- **Hackathons:** Challenge-based events co-organised with UN partner agencies
- **Youth Special Track:** Dedicated sessions for youth engagement
- **Regulators Roundtable:** Senior regulatory authority engagement on ICT policy and regulation

### WSIS Prizes

Launched in 2012, the WSIS Prizes contest is an integral part of the WSIS Stocktaking process. It recognises excellence in implementing WSIS Action Lines and SDGs. Key statistics:
- Over **9,000 projects** submitted for consideration since inception
- **252 winners** and **720 champions** recognised to date (as of 2025)
- Annual categories aligned to the 11 Action Lines
- Special prizes: WSIS Healthy Ageing Innovation Prize (launched 2021); WSIS Digital Service Design Prize (launched 2023, in collaboration with GovStack)
- Online voting engages hundreds of thousands of stakeholders globally
- Submissions come from all regions and stakeholder groups — governments, private sector, civil society, academia

### WSIS+20 High-Level Events (2024–2025)

The 2024 and 2025 editions took on special significance as preparatory events for the UNGA's WSIS+20 Overall Review scheduled for 16–17 December 2025.

**2024 (27–31 May, Geneva):**
- Co-hosted by ITU and the Swiss Confederation; co-organised with [[unesco]], [[undp]], [[unctad]]
- 4,000+ participants from 160 countries; 80+ ministers
- Chaired by H.E. Albert Rösti (Federal Councillor, Switzerland)
- Covered GDC, Summit of the Future, digital public infrastructure, AI governance, universal connectivity, cybersecurity
- Produced: Chair's Summary, Outcome Document, High-Level Outcomes and Executive Brief

**2025 (7–11 July, Palexpo Geneva):**
- Co-located with AI for Good Global Summit — first joint WSIS+AI for Good event
- 11,000+ participants from 169 countries; 59 ministers; 56 regulatory authority heads; 60+ UN representatives
- Chaired by H.E. Solly Malatsi (Minister of Communications, South Africa)
- Keynote: President of Estonia H.E. Alar Karis
- WSIS+20 Review co-facilitators (Kenya and Albania) held stakeholder consultations
- Emphasised: avoiding duplication with GDC, WSIS as cornerstone of digital development, need for clear Action Line targets, multistakeholder governance, human rights, sustainability
- Produced: Chair's Summary, Outcome Document, WSIS Stocktaking 2025 Global Report, WSIS Stocktaking+20 Report (zero draft)

## Connections
- Mandated by [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]] (implementation and follow-up framework)
- Hosted and convened by [[itu]]; co-organised with [[undp]], [[unesco]], [[unctad]]
- Reviews progress on all eleven Action Lines from [[geneva-plan-of-action]]
- WSIS Prizes align with WSIS Action Lines and [[sdgs]]
- 2024 and 2025 editions served as preparatory platforms for [[wsis-review-2025]] (UNGA WSIS+20 Overall Review, December 2025)
- Works in synergy with [[internet-governance-forum]] (many participants attend both)
- Covers [[global-digital-compact]] topics increasingly from 2024 onwards
- Reports to ECOSOC and CSTD in the UN system

## Open Questions
- How effective has the Forum's policy recommendations been in actually changing national and international digital policy?
- The shift from in-person to hybrid/virtual formats (accelerated by COVID-19) — has this genuinely broadened participation or mainly expanded virtual headcounts?
- How does the WSIS Forum avoid duplicating work being done at the [[internet-governance-forum]], ITU plenipotentiary cycles, and now [[global-digital-compact]] follow-up processes?
- The 2025 co-location with AI for Good Global Summit — a sign of convergence between the digital development and AI governance communities, or a logistical convenience?
