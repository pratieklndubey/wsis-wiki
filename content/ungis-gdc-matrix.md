# WSIS Process and 2030 Agenda – GDC Matrix (UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix)

## Overview
The **WSIS Process and 2030 Agenda – GDC Matrix** (commonly called the **UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix**) is the comprehensive three-way mapping document produced by [[ungis]] in 2024–2025, linking:
- Every commitment in the [[global-digital-compact]] (GDC, adopted September 2024)
- To relevant [[wsis-action-lines]] (C1–C11 and sub-themes)
- To relevant [[sdgs]] (SDGs 1–17)
- With specific existing UN mechanism examples for each link

Also titled "WSIS Process follow-up on the Summit of the Future Outcome Documents," the matrix was produced jointly by **28 UN agencies** and is the authoritative tool for demonstrating that GDC commitments are not duplicative of WSIS but complementary — with WSIS providing the established implementation machinery and the GDC providing updated governance architecture for new challenges (AI, data governance, digital public infrastructure).

The matrix is used directly in the [[gdc-implementation-map]] produced by the UN Working Group on Digital Technologies (WGDT) and is referenced throughout the WSIS+20 preparatory process and the outcome resolution [[unga-res-80-173]].

## Key Facts
- **Full title:** WSIS Process and 2030 Agenda – GDC Matrix / WSIS Process follow-up on the Summit of the Future Outcome Documents

## Detail

### Structure and Methodology

The matrix is structured around the GDC's 5 objectives and the follow-up/review section. For each GDC action point, the matrix provides:
1. **GDC text** — the exact paragraph from the Global Digital Compact
2. **SDG(s)** — the most relevant Sustainable Development Goals
3. **WSIS Action Lines** — the relevant C1–C11 Action Lines (and C7 sub-themes)
4. **Existing WSIS structures/mechanisms** — specific named UN programmes, platforms, reports, and initiatives already implementing this commitment

An introductory section identifies 8 key similarities between the WSIS Process and the Pact for the Future: Inclusivity, Addressing Digital Divide, International Cooperation, SDG Alignment, Capacity Building, Human Rights, Innovation/Knowledge Sharing, Ethical Technology.

### Objective 1: Close Digital Divides and Accelerate Progress on the SDGs

**Cross-cutting:** All SDGs | All 18 WSIS Action Lines

**Sub-theme: Connectivity** (a–g)
- Universal connectivity targets, blended financing, resilient infrastructure
- Schools and hospitals: Giga (ITU-UNICEF), Partner2Connect
- Sustainable and affordable infrastructure for vulnerable populations, gender mainstreaming
- Key mechanisms: Connect2Recover, Broadband Commission, EQUALS Partnership, Giga

**Sub-theme: Digital Literacy and Skills** (a–h)
- National skills strategies, platforms/software accessibility
- Targeted capacity building for marginalized groups
- Digital inclusion surveys, digital competencies for public officials
- Reskilling for automation, interoperable frameworks, STEM for women/girls
- Key mechanisms: ITU Academy, UNESCO ROAM-X indicators, UNDP Digital Readiness Assessment, EQUALS

**Sub-theme: Digital Public Goods** (a–f)
- (a) Open-source software, open data, open AI models, open standards → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Mechanisms: GovStack, AI for Good Open-Source, OpenWallet Forum, IGF, UNDP/FAO Digital Public Goods Alliance
- (b) Open standards and interoperability → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Mechanisms: GovStack, OpenWallet Forum, IGF, UNU (EGOV, IIGH)
- (c) Safeguards for inclusive/responsible/safe Digital Public Infrastructure → SDG 16 | C2, C3, C10, C7 E-Agriculture
  - Mechanisms: GovStack, UNDP Universal DPI Safeguards Framework, FAO-Digital Green FarmStack
- (d) Exchange best practices on DPI → SDGs 16 and 17 | Multiple Action Lines
  - Mechanisms: UNGIS, WSIS Forum, IGF, WSIS Stocktaking, WSIS Prizes

### Objective 3: Foster an Inclusive, Open, Safe and Secure Digital Space

**Cross-cutting:** All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines

**Sub-theme: Human Rights** (a–d)
- (a) National legislation compliant with international human rights law → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Key: OHCHR Human Rights Advisory Service on Digital Technologies, ILO labour platform standards
- (b) Safeguards against adverse human rights impacts → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Key: ITU-OHCHR collaboration, ITU-T standards with human rights lens, UNDP Universal DPI Safeguards
- (c) Rights of the child in digital space → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Key: ITU Child Online Protection Programme (COP), UNODC cybercrime programme
- (d) Refrain from restricting free flow of information → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines

**Sub-theme: Internet Governance** (a–d)
- (a) Open, global, interoperable Internet → SDG 9 | C1, C2, C3, C5, C6, C9, C11, C7 E-Agriculture
  - Key: IGF, WSIS Forum, COP, Internet Universality Indicators, draft UN Convention against Cybercrime
- (b) Support the [[internet-governance-forum]] → SDGs 9 and 10 | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Key: IGF, Arab IGF (ESCWA), UNESCO digital platform governance guidelines
- (c) Promote international cooperation against internet fragmentation → SDG 16 | All WSIS Action Lines
- (d) Refrain from internet shutdowns → SDG 16 | All WSIS Action Lines

**Sub-theme: Digital Trust and Safety** (a–f)
- (a) Safe, secure online space for mental health and well-being → SDGs 3, 5, 9, 10, 16 and 17 | C1, C2, C3, C5, C6, C7 E-Health, C7 E-Government, C9, C11
  - Key: WSIS Forum, ITU cybersecurity programme, UNODC cybercrime
- (b) National child online safety policies → SDGs 3, 5 and 10 | C2, C5, C6, C7 E-Health, C7 E-Government, C7 E-learning
- (e) Counter violence and abuse in the digital space → SDGs 5 and 16 | Multiple Action Lines

**Sub-theme: Information Integrity** (a–e)
- (a) Digital media and information literacy curricula → SDGs 3 and 4 | All WSIS Action Lines, C3, C7 E-learning, C9, C11
  - Key: UNESCO Recommendation on Ethics of AI, UNESCO work on Ethics
- (b) Diverse and resilient information ecosystems → SDGs 9 and 16 | All WSIS Action Lines, C9
  - Key: UNESCO Media and Ethics, UNDP Oslo Governance Center, UNDP iVerify
- (c–e) Counter misinformation/disinformation, crisis information access, UN assessment of disinformation impact → SDGs 3, 4, 9, 10, 16 | C9, C11

**Sub-theme: Data Exchanges and Standards** (a–c)

### Objective 5: Enhance International Governance of Artificial Intelligence

**Cross-cutting:** All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines

Key commitments (GDC paras. 55–62):
- **Para. 55–56(a):** Establish Independent International Scientific Panel on AI → SDG 17 | C2, C5, C10
  - Key: AI for Good multistakeholder platform, ITU GSR, Inter-agency AI working group, World Standards Cooperation (ITU, ISO, IEC)
- **Para. 56(b):** Initiate Global Dialogue on AI Governance → SDG 17 | C2, C5, C10
  - Key: AI for Good, WSIS Forum, IGF, ITU GSR, UNDP AI for Sustainable Development Hub
- **Para. 57:** Co-facilitators to identify terms of reference for AI Panel and Dialogue → SDG 17 | C2, C5, C10
- **Para. 58:** Interoperable AI standards that uphold safety/reliability/sustainability/human rights → SDGs 3, 5, 7, 9, 10, 12, 16 and 17 | C2, C5, C10
  - Key: UNJSPF ISO AI certification (ISO 42001), World Standards Cooperation, International AI Standards Summit
- **Para. 59:** Safe, trustworthy AI preserving linguistic/cultural diversity and multilingualism → SDGs 10 and 16 | C2, C5, C8
  - Key: Global AI Ethics and Governance Observatory (UNESCO, ITU), UNESCO RAM, UNESCO EIA
- **Para. 60:** International AI partnerships for education, open models, training data → SDGs 4 and 17 | C2, C3, C4, C5, C10, C11
- **Para. 61:** Leverage UN mechanisms to bridge AI divides → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines
  - Key: AI for Good, UNDP AI for Sustainable Development Hub, FAO Digital Agriculture Innovation Hubs
- **Para. 62:** North-South/South-South cooperation for representative datasets, affordable compute → SDGs 4, 9, 10 and 17 | C3, C8, C11

### Follow-up and Review Section (GDC paras. 64–74)

The matrix also maps the GDC's follow-up provisions to WSIS structures:
- **Para. 64:** Implement GDC at national, regional, global levels → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines | WSIS Outcome Documents, CSTD, UN Agency resolutions
- **Para. 68:** Build on WSIS processes (IGF, WSIS Forum) for GDC implementation → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines | IGF (173 NRIs), WSIS+20 High-Level Event (7–11 July 2025)
- **Para. 70:** CSTD role in reviewing GDC/WSIS implementation → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines | CSTD, WSIS Action Line Facilitation, IGF
- **Para. 71:** SG implementation map → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines | WSIS Stocktaking, CSTD, WSIS Forum, IGF
- **Para. 72:** System-wide coordination via new office (ODET) → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines | UNGIS, Inter-agency AI WG, CSTD
- **Para. 74:** High-Level Review at 82nd GA → All SDGs | All WSIS Action Lines | UNGIS, CSTD, WSIS Forum, IGF

## Open Questions
- How often will the matrix be updated — is a biennial revision cycle planned under UNGIS?
- Does the matrix capture genuinely new WSIS activities catalysed by the GDC, or only pre-existing activities that happen to match GDC language?
- The AI governance section (Objective 5) relies heavily on AI for Good — how does ITU manage the tension between its normative WSIS role and its commercial/event role through AI for Good?
- How does the matrix handle GDC provisions where no existing WSIS mechanism exists?
