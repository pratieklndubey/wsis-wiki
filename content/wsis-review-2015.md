# WSIS+10 Review (2015)

## Overview
The WSIS+10 Review was the first formal overall review of the implementation of the World Summit on the Information Society outcomes, held ten years after the close of [[wsis-phase-ii-tunis-2005]]. It was mandated directly by paragraph 111 of the [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]], which called on the UN General Assembly (UNGA) to make an overall review of WSIS outcomes in 2015.

The review process culminated in a **High-Level Meeting of the General Assembly** in December 2015, which adopted **UNGA Resolution A/RES/70/125** — the key outcome document reaffirming the WSIS framework, aligning it with the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development and the newly adopted Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and extending WSIS implementation guidance through 2025. The resolution also called for a further overall review in 2025 (WSIS+20).

A major preparatory milestone was the **WSIS+10 High-Level Event** (Geneva, June 2014), co-organised by [[itu]], [[unesco]], [[unctad]], and [[undp]], which produced two consensus documents: the WSIS+10 Statement on the Implementation of WSIS Outcomes and the WSIS+10 Vision for WSIS Beyond 2015.

- **UNGA modalities resolution:** A/RES/68/302 (adopted July 2014)

## Detail

### Mandate and Background

Paragraph 111 of the [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]] (2005) explicitly requested the UNGA to make an "overall review of the implementation of WSIS outcomes in 2015." This was one of the clearest long-term mandates in the Tunis Agenda, providing a ten-year horizon for accountability.

By 2013–2014, the global digital landscape had transformed dramatically from 2005: Internet usage had expanded from approximately 16% in 2005 to over 40% by 2014; mobile subscriptions had surged to over 7 billion; broadband infrastructure had expanded across developing regions. Yet persistent digital divides remained, and new challenges — including cybersecurity, data privacy, the governance of platforms, and the rise of mobile Internet — demanded attention.

### WSIS+10 High-Level Event (2014)

Ahead of the UNGA review, a preparatory WSIS+10 High-Level Event was co-organised in Geneva from 10–13 June 2014. This served as the multistakeholder input process for the UNGA review, reviewing progress across all WSIS Action Lines based on submissions from countries, Action Line Facilitators, and other stakeholders.

The event produced two consensus documents through the Multistakeholder Preparatory Platform (MPP):
1. **[[wsis-plus10-statement]]** — assessed achievements across all 11 Action Lines, identified 30 specific challenges for the next decade (including new themes: e-waste, media literacy, ethics of emerging technologies, privacy post-Snowden), and reaffirmed that "the Geneva Declaration and Geneva Plan of Action still remain valid until further decisions by the General Assembly"
2. **WSIS+10 Vision for WSIS Beyond 2015** ([[wsis-plus10-vision]]) — articulated a forward-looking vision stressing alignment with the forthcoming SDGs and the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development

A defining feature of the [[wsis-plus10-statement]] was its deliberate scope restriction: it covered only the Geneva Plan of Action Action Lines and explicitly avoided addressing Tunis-phase outcomes (Internet governance, IGF, enhanced cooperation, CSTD), which were reserved for the UNGA modalities process. The document was a multistakeholder consensus document — not an intergovernmental resolution — developed under ITU Council Resolution 1334.

### UNGA Resolution A/RES/70/125 (December 2015)

The culminating outcome of the WSIS+10 Review. Key provisions:
- **Reaffirmed** all stakeholders' commitment to the WSIS process and its Action Lines as the core framework for digital development
- **Aligned WSIS** with the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development and the SDGs (adopted by UNGA September 2015), calling on all stakeholders to integrate ICTs into their SDG implementation approaches
- **Requested** UN entities facilitating WSIS Action Lines to review their reporting and work plans to support the 2030 Agenda (para. 12)
- **Called for close alignment** between the WSIS process and the 2030 Agenda (para. 5)
- **Called upon** UN Regional Commissions to continue their work in WSIS Action Line implementation and contribute to reviews
- **Called for** a new UNGA high-level overall review of WSIS outcomes in 2025 (WSIS+20)
- **Extended** WSIS implementation guidance through 2025

### WSIS-SDG Matrix

At the WSIS Forum 2015, the UN Action Line Facilitators launched the **WSIS-SDG Matrix** — a tool that clearly maps each of the 11 WSIS Action Lines to the 17 SDGs. This provided:
- A framework for assessing how ICT initiatives contribute to specific SDGs
- Rationale for each WSIS-SDG linkage
- A guide for stakeholders to align WSIS reporting with SDG progress reporting

Key mappings include: Action Line C2 (Infrastructure) → SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, Infrastructure); C4 (Capacity Building) → SDG 4 (Quality Education); C5 (Cybersecurity) → SDG 16 (Peace, Justice, Strong Institutions); C11 (International Cooperation) → SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals).

### Achievements Highlighted by WSIS+10

The [[wsis-plus10-statement]] documented 22 specific achievements across the Action Lines (Section B). Key highlights:
- **Connectivity growth:** Global Internet usage roughly tripled from 2003 to 2015; mobile broadband subscriptions surged
- **Mobile and social media:** WSIS+10 Statement was the first WSIS outcome document to explicitly mention social media and cloud computing as access platforms
- **Enabling environment:** Recognition that "policies, content, and capacities are as important as infrastructure" — a significant shift from infrastructure-first assumptions
- **WSIS Forum:** Explicitly recognised as "an efficient global multistakeholder platform" for coordinating WSIS implementation
- **WSIS Stocktaking and Prizes:** Recognised as "an efficient and effective platform for collection of ICT-related projects" and sharing best practices globally
- **ICT indicator framework:** Partnership on Measuring ICT for Development and the ICT Development Index (IDI) adopted by UN Statistical Commission
- **National digital strategies:** International, regional, and national strategies for inclusive Information Society developed across many countries

### Persistent Challenges

The [[wsis-plus10-statement]] Section C identified 30 specific challenges requiring continued attention. Key themes:

- **Digital divide still inadequately addressed** (Challenge 5): More than half the world's population still not connected; infrastructure, capacity, and local content gaps especially in rural and remote areas
- **Affordability** (Challenge 7): Particularly in developing countries and LDCs; equity of access between urban/rural and between countries
- **Human rights online/offline** (Challenge 1): Protect and reinforce all human rights; ensure equal respect and enforcement **online and offline** — a significant post-Snowden formulation
- **Gender equality** (Challenge 2): Fully integrate gender equality perspectives in WSIS strategies
- **Privacy** (Challenge 30): Promote further dialogue on protection of privacy in light of technological developments — the most direct WSIS reference to post-Snowden concerns
- **Skills and technology gaps** (Challenges 18–19): Both gaps growing between rich and poor within and between countries
- **New challenges not in original WSIS documents:** E-waste (Challenge 25), media and information literacy (Challenge 27), ethics of emerging technologies (Challenge 28)

## Open Questions
- Did Resolution A/RES/70/125's call for WSIS-SDG alignment produce genuine integration in national ICT strategies, or was it mainly aspirational?
- The WSIS-SDG Matrix was developed by UN facilitators — was it adopted and used by non-UN stakeholders in their planning?
- How was the WSIS+10 process related to the SDG negotiation process, which was also underway in 2014–2015? Was there coordination?
- The WSIS+10 Vision for WSIS Beyond 2015 — how much of it was reflected in the WSIS+20 review inputs?
