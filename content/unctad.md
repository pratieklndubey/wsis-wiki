# United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD)

## Overview
UNCTAD (the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development) is the principal UN body dealing with trade, investment, and development issues, particularly from the perspective of developing countries. Founded in **1964**, it is headquartered at the **Palais des Nations, Geneva**, alongside [[itu]] and other Geneva-based UN bodies. Its current Secretary-General is **Rebeca Grynspan** (Costa Rica, since 2021).

Within the WSIS ecosystem, UNCTAD plays several critical roles: it is a co-organiser of the annual [[wsis-forum]], a co-facilitator of Action Lines C4 and C6, provides the secretariat for the **Commission on Science and Technology for Development (CSTD)** — the primary UN intergovernmental body for WSIS follow-up — and currently **chairs [[ungis]]** (as of 2024/2025). UNCTAD's **Digital Economy Report** (formerly the Information Economy Report) is a key annual publication tracking global digital economy trends.

## Key Facts
- **Full name:** United Nations Conference on Trade and Development
- **Founded:** 30 December 1964 (UNGA resolution)
- **Headquarters:** Palais des Nations, Geneva, Switzerland
- **Secretary-General:** Rebeca Grynspan (Costa Rica), since 11 June 2021
- **Members:** 195 state members (all UN members plus observer states, Holy See, Palestine)
- **Staff:** ~400
- **Budget:** ~$138 million biannual (core) + ~$72 million extra-budgetary technical assistance
- **Governance:** Reports to UNGA and ECOSOC; UNCTAD Conference (quadrennial); Trade and Development Board (annual); four commissions
- **WSIS roles:**
  - **C4** (Capacity Building) — co-facilitator
  - **C6** (Enabling Environment) — co-facilitator
  - **C7 E-Business** — co-facilitator (with WTO, ITU, UPU)
  - **C7 E-Science** — co-facilitator (with UNESCO, ITU)
- **CSTD:** Provides secretariat for the Commission on Science and Technology for Development
- **UNGIS:** Current Chair (2024/2025); permanent member
- **WSIS Forum:** Co-organiser alongside [[itu]], [[undp]], [[unesco]]
- **Key publication:** Digital Economy Report (annual; formerly Information Economy Report)

## Detail

### History and Mandate

UNCTAD was established in 1964 because developing nations felt that existing institutions (GATT, IMF, World Bank) inadequately addressed their trade and development concerns. It was instrumental in developing the **Generalized System of Preferences (GSP)** — the first multilateral trade mechanism that granted preferential market access to developing country exports.

UNCTAD's mandate covers:
- Trade in goods, services, and commodities
- Investment, enterprise, and technology
- Macroeconomic policies, debt, and financing for development
- Digital economy and digital trade
- Competition policy and consumer protection

### UNCTAD's WSIS Role

**C4 (Capacity Building) — co-facilitator:** UNCTAD co-facilitates C4 alongside ITU (lead), UNESCO, UNDP, UN DESA, FAO, and UNIDO. The 16th facilitation meeting was held virtually in April 2021. UNCTAD's contribution focuses on ICT capacity building for trade and development.

**C6 (Enabling Environment) — co-facilitator:** UNCTAD co-facilitates C6 (UNDP leads) alongside ITU, UN Regional Commissions, UN DESA, UNIDO, and APC. UNCTAD's specific contribution covers e-commerce legal frameworks, competition policy, and consumer protection — aligning with its core mandate.

**C7 E-Business — co-facilitator:** UNCTAD co-facilitates the E-Business sub-theme of C7 (alongside WTO, ITU, and UPU). Focal point: Scarlett Fondeur Gil (UNCTAD). This is UNCTAD's most substantive WSIS facilitation role, drawing on its digital trade and e-commerce mandate.

**C7 E-Science — co-facilitator:** UNCTAD contributes to the E-Science sub-theme alongside UNESCO (lead) and ITU.

### CSTD Secretariat

UNCTAD provides the secretariat for the **Commission on Science and Technology for Development (CSTD)**, the ECOSOC subsidiary body that serves as the primary UN intergovernmental review mechanism for WSIS implementation. This makes UNCTAD institutionally central to WSIS accountability — the body that processes annual progress reports, prepares CSTD sessions, and coordinates CSTD's interactions with the UNGA.

The CSTD 29th session (2026) will receive the joint WSIS-GDC implementation roadmap from UNGIS; the CSTD 30th session (2027) will receive Action Line implementation roadmaps and the ITU/UNGIS financing task force report. UNCTAD will organise both sessions.

### UNGIS Chair

UNCTAD currently chairs [[ungis]], the inter-agency coordination mechanism for WSIS implementation. This role is rotating (other past chairs have included ITU, UNDP, etc.), but holding both the UNGIS Chair and the CSTD secretariat simultaneously gives UNCTAD considerable influence over the post-2025 WSIS institutional process.

### Digital Economy Report

UNCTAD's **Digital Economy Report** (published annually; formerly the *Information Economy Report* until 2019) is the most comprehensive UN analysis of digital economy trends for developing countries. Key themes include:

- Digital trade and cross-border data flows
- E-commerce development in developing countries
- Digital platforms and market concentration
- AI and automation impacts on development
- Data governance and digital sovereignty

This publication is directly relevant to WSIS Action Lines C6 (Enabling Environment) and C7 E-Business, and is a key input to WSIS Forum and CSTD deliberations.

### Technical Cooperation Programmes

UNCTAD's key technical assistance programmes include:
- **ASYCUDA** (Automated System for Customs Data) — used in 100+ countries
- **DMFAS** (Debt Management and Financial Analysis System)
- **EMPRETEC** — entrepreneurship development
- **UNCTAD Train for Trade** — trade facilitation capacity building
- **e-Commerce and Digital Economy Programme** — assists developing countries in formulating digital trade policies
