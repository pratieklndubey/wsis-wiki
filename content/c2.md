# C2: Information and Communication Infrastructure

## Overview
Action Line C2 is the physical and technical backbone of the WSIS framework, covering the deployment, management, and governance of information and communication infrastructure — from broadband networks and internet exchange points to spectrum management and satellite connectivity. It is consistently described as "central to achieving the goal of digital inclusion" and underpins virtually all other Action Lines: no e-government, e-health, e-learning, or e-agriculture can function without infrastructure.

C2 was defined in Section C of the [[geneva-plan-of-action]] (12 December 2003) and assigned to [[itu]] as sole facilitator — the only Action Line with a single lead and no co-facilitating UN agency, reflecting ITU's unique technical mandate over telecommunications infrastructure. APC (Association for Progressive Communications) has served as civil society co-facilitator.

Over two decades, C2 has evolved from a focus on basic telephone and internet access to encompass broadband, 5G, internet of things (IoT), satellite mega-constellations, and the digital infrastructure needed for AI. It is the Action Line most directly measured by the WSIS connectivity targets — connecting schools, villages, hospitals, and libraries to ICTs by 2015 — which have since been updated and integrated into SDG 9.c.

## Detail

### Scope and Purpose

C2 addresses five major infrastructure dimensions:

**1. Universal access:** Closing the [[digital-divide]] through connectivity for all — with particular attention to rural, remote, and underserved communities. The WSIS Geneva targets called for village connectivity and community access points as the baseline floor.

**2. Broadband and backbone infrastructure:** Promoting high-speed broadband networks; inter-regional and intra-regional backbone connectivity; internet exchange points (IXPs) to reduce international traffic costs for developing countries.

**3. Spectrum management:** Radio frequency spectrum as a public resource; equitable access to spectrum for developing countries; terrestrial and satellite spectrum coordination.

**4. Universal access/service:** Policy frameworks — universal access funds, regulatory obligations — that extend connectivity to commercially unviable areas. ITU's work on enabling regulatory environments and competitive markets is the primary operational vehicle.

**5. Satellite and emerging technologies:** Satellite services for remote connectivity; LEO constellation services (Starlink, OneWeb, etc.); 5G; next-generation networks. By 2025, C2 facilitation had incorporated AI infrastructure (data centre energy consumption, compute access) as an emerging theme.

### Key Geneva Plan of Action Provisions

The C2 section instructed:
- Universal, sustainable, ubiquitous, and affordable access to ICTs
- Broadband network deployment, particularly in developing countries
- Promotion of satellite services for connectivity in remote areas
- Radio spectrum management that is equitable and efficient
- Development of regional ICT backbone networks and IXPs
- Development of ICT standards that are accessible and affordable
- Promotion of wireless technologies and community access points

### Connectivity Targets: Progress After 20 Years

The WSIS Geneva targets for connectivity — one of the most concrete WSIS benchmarks — were reviewed in the [[wsis-review-2025]] (2025):

| Target | 2003 Baseline | 2024 Status |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Connect villages | 16% online globally | 68% online (5.5 billion); 9B+ mobile broadband subscriptions |
| Connect schools | Limited data | 40% primary; 50% lower secondary; 65% upper secondary connected |
| Connect libraries | Limited data | 369,000+ with internet; 87% still offline |
| Connect health centres | Limited data | EHR mainly in developed countries; significant rural–urban gap |
| Half the world online | 16% | 68% online; 96% with mobile broadband coverage |

Despite progress, 2.6 billion people remain offline in 2024, with 1.8 billion in rural areas. The gender gap persists: 189 million more men than women online globally.

### SDG Connections

C2's infrastructure focus connects most directly to SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, Infrastructure):
- **SDG 1.4** (Access to financial services, ICT): Digital financial infrastructure as a poverty tool
- **SDG 8.2** (Economic productivity): ICT infrastructure as productivity driver
- **SDG 9.1** (Infrastructure development): Reliable, sustainable infrastructure for all
- **SDG 9.a** (Facilitate sustainable infrastructure in developing countries): ODA and south-south infrastructure investment
- **SDG 9.c** ("Significantly increase access to ICT and strive to provide universal and affordable access to the Internet in LDCs by 2020"): The single SDG target most directly matching the WSIS connectivity agenda — C2's primary SDG link
- **SDG 11.5** (Disaster resilience): Communications infrastructure for disaster warning and response
- **SDG 11.b** (Resilient cities): Smart city ICT infrastructure

### WSIS+20 Mandate (2025)

[[unga-res-80-173]] (December 2025) noted major provisions for infrastructure:
- **Para. 18:** Reaffirmed C2 and all Action Lines as "the main framework"
- **Para. 30–35** (Connectivity section): Called for enhanced efforts to connect the remaining 2.6 billion; emphasised gender equity in connectivity; recognised rural connectivity gaps; welcomed ITU's Giga initiative (connecting schools)
- **Para. 113:** C2 facilitators to develop implementation roadmap by CSTD 2027
- **Paras. 42–47** (Environment): New thematic provisions on energy consumption of data centre and AI infrastructure — a new dimension for C2

### Facilitation Meeting History

16 facilitation meetings were held under C2 (2006–2021), held at the [[wsis-forum]] in Geneva (primarily) and occasionally other venues. Each meeting produced an outcome document feeding into WSIS Forum proceedings. Meeting themes evolved from basic connectivity (2006–2010) through broadband and mobile (2011–2015), to 5G, AI, and sustainable infrastructure (2016–2021). From 2020–2021, meetings were held virtually due to COVID-19.

The C2 virtual working space on the ITU WSIS portal serves as a collaborative platform for stakeholder engagement between meetings, including a C2 Virtual Group for coordinating participants.

## Open Questions
- The original 2015 targets for connectivity have mostly been reframed into SDG 9.c — is this an adequate successor framework, or did important nuances of the WSIS targets get lost?
- Satellite mega-constellations (Starlink, OneWeb) were not contemplated in 2003 — how has C2 facilitation adapted to address their regulatory implications, including orbital congestion and spectrum interference?
- Energy consumption by data centres and AI infrastructure is now a major sustainability concern — does C2's mandate adequately cover this, or is it primarily a C7 E-Environment issue?
- Has the 16-meeting facilitation process for C2 produced measurable changes in policy, or is it primarily a dialogue forum?
