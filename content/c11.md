# C11: International and Regional Cooperation

## Overview
Action Line C11 is the partnership and cooperation pillar of the WSIS framework, addressing the international and regional mechanisms through which the WSIS vision is implemented across borders. It calls for strengthening North-South, South-South, and triangular cooperation on ICT development; mainstreaming ICTs in official development assistance; building regional ICT cooperation frameworks; and ensuring that international ICT governance reflects the needs and voices of all countries, not only the most powerful.

C11 is co-facilitated by the UN Regional Commissions, [[undp]], [[itu]], [[unesco]], and ECOSOC — a coalition that reflects the geographic and institutional breadth of international cooperation. The current lead contact (Deniz Susar, UN DESA) also leads C1 (Government Roles) and C7 E-Government, creating a cluster of governance and cooperation work at UN DESA.

C11 maps primarily to SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals), which is itself the "means of implementation" SDG — the financing, technology, capacity, and systemic framework commitments that enable all other SDGs. This makes C11 the Action Line most directly aligned with the SDG architecture's foundational assumption that development requires international partnership, not unilateral action.

## Key Facts
- **Full name:** International and Regional Cooperation
- **Code:** C11 (Action Line 11)
- **Origin:** [[geneva-plan-of-action]], Section C, para. C11 (adopted 12 December 2003)
- **Facilitating organisations:** UN Regional Commissions / [[undp]] / [[itu]] / [[unesco]] / ECOSOC
- **Current lead contact:** Deniz Susar, Governance and Public Administration Officer, UN DESA
- **Related SDGs:** 17 — *international partnership as the primary implementation mechanism*
- **WSIS-SDG Matrix targets:** 17.9 (capacity building); 17.16 (multi-stakeholder partnerships for sustainable development); 17.17 (effective partnerships)
- **Facilitation meetings:** Annual at [[wsis-forum]] since 2006 (data not published separately on ITU facilitation pages)
- **WSIS+20 mandate:** Implementation roadmap linking C11 to SDG targets and [[global-digital-compact]] commitments, due to CSTD 30th session (2027)

## Detail

### Scope and Purpose

C11 addresses five major dimensions of international and regional cooperation:

**1. ODA mainstreaming:** Calling on bilateral and multilateral development donors to mainstream ICTs in their development assistance programmes. WSIS argued that ICT should not be a separate silo within development cooperation but integrated across health, education, agriculture, and governance assistance.

**2. South-South cooperation:** Promoting ICT cooperation among developing countries — sharing knowledge, technology, and policy experience across the Global South. This responds to the recognition that developing-country experiences with ICT-for-development are often more directly applicable to other developing countries than Northern models.

**3. Regional integration:** Supporting regional ICT cooperation frameworks — regional broadband markets, spectrum harmonisation, regional data protection frameworks, regional regulatory bodies. The five UN Regional Commissions (ECA, ECE, ECLAC, ESCAP, ESCWA) are C11 co-facilitators precisely because regional integration is their core mandate.

**4. International financing for ICT:** Working with international financial institutions (World Bank, regional development banks) to increase ICT lending; advocating for ICT infrastructure in global financing frameworks. This overlaps with the [[digital-solidarity-fund]] concept from Section D of the [[geneva-plan-of-action]].

**5. Progress evaluation:** Establishing international mechanisms to evaluate WSIS implementation; developing ICT indicators; supporting statistical capacity in developing countries.

### Key Geneva Plan of Action Provisions

The C11 section called for:
- Strengthening existing forms of international and regional cooperation on ICT for development
- Mainstreaming ICT-related development goals in strategies of international organisations
- Promoting joint ICT projects at regional level — particularly backbone infrastructure
- Developing the regional component of the WSIS process (regional preparatory meetings)
- Supporting the UN Global Compact's role in promoting responsible ICT business practices
- Evaluating progress through quantitative and qualitative indicators

### The Regional Commissions' Role

The five UN Regional Commissions are central to C11:
- **UNECA** (Africa): Pan-African e-Agenda; African Union digital strategy; Connect Africa
- **UNECE** (Europe): Digital policy dialogue among UNECE members; transport ICT
- **ECLAC** (Latin America and Caribbean): Digital Agenda for Latin America and the Caribbean (eLAC); regional digital indicators
- **ESCAP** (Asia-Pacific): Asia-Pacific Information Superhighway; regional broadband
- **ESCWA** (Arab States): Arab Information and Communication Technology Organization (AICTO); Arab ICT indicators

These regional bodies organised the regional preparatory meetings for both WSIS phases (2002–2005) and for WSIS+20 (2023–2025), feeding regional perspectives into global processes.

### South-South Cooperation in Practice

South-South ICT cooperation has grown substantially since 2003:
- **India:** Provides e-governance software and training to African and Pacific countries (C-DAC, NIC)
- **China:** Digital Silk Road investments in developing-country ICT infrastructure
- **Brazil:** Open-source government software shared with Portuguese-speaking countries
- **Estonia:** E-governance expertise shared through the Tallinn Digital Agenda
- **Korea:** KOICA's ICT-for-development programmes in Asia and Africa

The ITU Development Sector (ITU-D) is the primary multilateral vehicle for C11 technical assistance, providing spectrum management, regulatory training, and connectivity project support to developing-country members.

### SDG Connections

C11 maps primarily to SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals):
- **SDG 17.9** (Capacity building for developing countries): Technical assistance and knowledge sharing; ITU-D programme
- **SDG 17.16** (Enhance the Global Partnership for Sustainable Development): Multi-stakeholder ICT partnerships; WSIS Forum as partnership platform
- **SDG 17.17** (Encourage effective partnerships): Public-private-civil society ICT partnerships

C11's SDG connection is the narrowest of any Action Line — but SDG 17 is also the most foundational, as it is the "means of implementation" goal that enables all others.

### WSIS+20 Mandate (2025)

[[unga-res-80-173]] (December 2025) addressed international cooperation extensively:
- **Para. 18:** Reaffirmed C11 and all Action Lines as "the main framework"
- **Para. 56–65** (Financing for development): Called for enhanced ODA for digital development in LDCs and SIDS; recognised the role of multilateral development banks in ICT financing; called for innovative financing mechanisms
- **Para. 88–100** (Multistakeholder governance and IGF): Reaffirmed multistakeholder cooperation; made IGF permanent; called for enhanced participation of developing countries in global internet governance
- **Para. 113:** C11 facilitators to develop implementation roadmap by CSTD 2027
- **Para. 124:** Biennial WSIS progress reports to be submitted to CSTD — a new reporting mechanism that will require C11 cooperative data collection

### Facilitation Meeting History

C11 facilitation meetings are held annually at [[wsis-forum]], coordinated by the five UN Regional Commissions, [[undp]], [[itu]], [[unesco]], and ECOSOC. The meetings serve as a coordination platform for international and regional ICT cooperation commitments. Data on C11 meeting counts was not accessible from ITU's C11 facilitation page (the page returned navigation-only content), but facilitation has been continuous since 2006.

## Connections
- Defined in [[geneva-plan-of-action]] Section C (2003); facilitators confirmed in [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]] Annex (2005)
- Broadest facilitating coalition after C7: five Regional Commissions + UNDP + ITU + UNESCO + ECOSOC
- Linked to [[c1]] (Government roles): C11 is C1's international dimension
- Linked to [[c6]] (Enabling Environment): international regulatory harmonisation is a C6 and C11 shared concern
- [[digital-solidarity-fund]] is the dedicated financing mechanism for C11 goals
- SDG 17 (Partnerships) is the primary SDG alignment — C11 as the "means of implementation" action line
- [[global-digital-compact]] (2024) explicitly calls for enhanced international cooperation for digital development — directly reinforcing C11
- [[ungis]] coordinates C11 work at the UN system level
- Facilitation meetings held annually at [[wsis-forum]]; five Regional Commissions organise regional preparatory events

## Open Questions
- C11 calls for "mainstreaming ICTs in ODA" — 20 years later, has this actually happened? What share of ODA goes to ICT development?
- South-South ICT cooperation has grown, but China's Digital Silk Road raises concerns about infrastructure dependency and data sovereignty — how should C11 address this?
- The biennial WSIS progress reporting mechanism (mandated by A/RES/80/173, para. 124) will require international data collection — is the C11 coordination mechanism adequate for this?
- Should C11 be more explicitly linked to the GDC's international cooperation commitments, which cover overlapping ground on digital development financing?
