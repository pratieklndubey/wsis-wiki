# WSIS-SDG Matrix

## Overview
The **WSIS-SDG Matrix** is the official cross-reference tool linking the eleven [[wsis-action-lines]] (C1–C11, plus C7 sub-themes) to specific targets within the 17 Sustainable Development Goals ([[sdgs]]). It was produced by UN Action Line facilitators and publicly launched at the **WSIS Forum 2015** (25–29 May 2015, Geneva, Switzerland), coinciding with the finalization of the 2030 Agenda.

The matrix provides a three-layer structure: (1) a high-level grid showing which Action Lines connect to which SDGs, (2) a goal-by-goal listing of relevant Action Lines for each of the 17 SDGs, and (3) a target-level rationale explaining each specific link in the Annex. The document establishes the formal institutional basis for WSIS being understood as an implementation mechanism for the SDGs — a relationship later reaffirmed by the WSIS+10 review (A/RES/70/125) and the WSIS+20 outcome ([[unga-res-80-173]], paras. 111–113).

## Key Facts
- **Title:** WSIS-SDG Matrix: Linking WSIS Action Lines with Sustainable Development Goals
- **Produced:** WSIS Forum 2015, 25–29 May 2015, Geneva, Switzerland
- **Producers:** UN Action Line facilitators — ITU, UNESCO, UNDP, UNCTAD, UNDESA, FAO, WHO, UPU, UNEP, ITC, ILO, WMO
- **Sections:** Section I (full grid), Section II (SDGs to Action Lines), Section III (Action Lines to SDGs), Annex I (detailed rationale), Annex II (full SDGs list), Annex III (ICT indicators for SDG framework)
- **Key finding:** C3 (Access to Information and Knowledge) links to all 17 SDGs — the most broadly connected Action Line
- **WSIS+20 mandate:** Para. 113 of [[unga-res-80-173]] requires facilitators to develop expanded roadmaps linking Action Lines to SDG targets with specific indicators by CSTD 2027

## Detail

### Methodology

The WSIS-SDG Matrix was developed through a consultative process among all UN Action Line facilitators. Each facilitating agency identified all plausible and substantive linkages between the Action Lines they facilitate and the SDG targets. These were then consolidated into a cross-reference matrix and detailed rationale document.

The methodology is bottom-up: subject-matter experts within UN agencies (ITU for infrastructure/security, UNESCO for culture/education, WHO for health, FAO for agriculture, etc.) contributed specific target-level justifications, rather than the matrix being constructed top-down from a single institutional perspective.

### Section I: Full Grid (Action Lines × SDGs)

The master grid shows all 18 WSIS Action Line variants (C1, C2, C3, C4, C5, C6, C7-EGov, C7-EB, C7-EL, C7-EH, C7-EEmp, C7-EEnv, C7-EAg, C7-ESci, C8, C9, C10, C11) against all 17 SDGs. The colour-coded grid shows:
- Multiple Action Lines intersecting with virtually every SDG
- C3 is unique in intersecting all 17 SDGs
- SDG 17 (Partnerships) intersects with all Action Lines

### Annex I: Detailed Rationale (C1–C11)

The Annex provides written rationale for each link. Key highlights from the rationale:

**C1–C3 rationale (pages 7–20):**
- C1's connection to SDG 16 (governance) is grounded in the overlap between national digital strategies, transparency, accountability, and public participation in governance
- C2's connection to SDG 9.c is the most direct: the Action Line literally addresses broadband infrastructure and universal access, matching the target's text
- C3's universal SDG connection rests on the principle that access to information is a prerequisite for informed action on poverty, health, education, gender equality, and every other development domain

### ICT Indicators for the SDG Framework (Annex III)

Annex III documents ICT-specific indicators proposed for the SDG global indicator framework. These include:
- Proportion of individuals using the Internet (SDG 9.c indicator)
- Fixed broadband subscriptions per 100 inhabitants
- Mobile broadband subscriptions per 100 inhabitants
- ICT Development Index (IDI) components

These indicators, maintained by [[itu]], provide the measurement backbone for tracking SDG 9.c progress.

### Updates and Subsequent Versions

The WSIS-SDG Matrix has been updated since its 2015 launch as:
1. The SDG global indicator framework was finalized (2017)
2. New ICT measurement methodologies were developed
3. The [[ungis-gdc-matrix]] (2024) extended the framework to include GDC objectives as a third dimension

The [[unga-res-80-173]] (WSIS+20, 2025) para. 113 mandates that Action Line facilitators produce expanded implementation roadmaps with "targets, indicators, and metrics" linking to both SDG targets and GDC commitments by CSTD 2027 — effectively requiring a substantially more granular version of this matrix.

## Open Questions
- Has the WSIS-SDG Matrix been formally updated since 2015, or does the 2027 roadmap mandate represent the first substantive revision?
- Some mappings (especially C3 to all 17 SDGs) are controversial — is the matrix intended as aspirational or as a measurable framework?
- Annex III ICT indicators: how many were officially adopted into the global SDG indicator framework vs. remaining as supplementary measures?
- After 2030: if the SDGs are replaced by a new development framework, how will the WSIS Action Lines be realigned?
