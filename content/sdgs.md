# Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

## Overview
The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are the 17 interconnected global goals adopted by all 193 UN Member States in **September 2015**, forming the heart of the **2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development**. They replaced the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs, 2000–2015) and represent the most comprehensive and ambitious development framework ever adopted by the UN. The SDGs are structured as a "shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into the future."

The SDGs and the WSIS [[wsis-action-lines]] are formally aligned: ICT is understood as both a cross-cutting enabler across all 17 goals and a specific focus of several (especially SDG 4, 9, 16, and 17). The [[wsis-review-2015]] outcome (A/RES/70/125) explicitly positioned WSIS as a key implementation mechanism for the SDGs. The **WSIS-SDG Matrix** (developed by UN Action Line facilitators, launched at WSIS Forum 2015) maps each WSIS Action Line to specific SDG targets.

## Key Facts
- **Adopted:** 25 September 2015, UN General Assembly (A/RES/70/1, "Transforming our world: the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development")
- **Agenda:** 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development
- **Number of goals:** 17
- **Number of targets:** 169
- **5 P's:** People, Planet, Prosperity, Peace, Partnership
- **Predecessor:** Millennium Development Goals (MDGs), 2000–2015
- **Review mechanism:** High-Level Political Forum on Sustainable Development (HLPF), annual UN mechanism
- **Technology Facilitation Mechanism (TFM):** Established by para. 70 of A/RES/70/1; multi-stakeholder body; online platform for STI for SDGs; annual STI Forum
- **WSIS-SDG Matrix:** Launched WSIS Forum 2015 (see [[wsis-sdg-matrix]]); maps all 11 [[wsis-action-lines]] (C1–C11 with sub-themes) to SDG targets; C3 (Access to Information and Knowledge) is linked to all 17 SDGs
- **WSIS+20 mandate:** Action line facilitators to develop implementation roadmaps linking C1–C11 to SDG targets and [[global-digital-compact]] commitments by CSTD 2027 ([[unga-res-80-173]], para. 113)
- **ICT-SDG links:** SDG 9.c (universal access); SDG 4 (education); SDG 16.10 (access to information); SDG 17 (partnerships for implementation) — most directly linked to WSIS
- **ICT explicit mentions:** Para. 15 of A/RES/70/1 (bridge digital divide and knowledge societies); SDG 9.c (universal affordable ICT access); SDG 5.b (ICT to empower women); SDG 17.8 (enable use of ICT for LDCs)

### Complete SDG Target Reference (Goals 10–17)

From the official text (A/RES/70/1):

**Goal 10 — Reduce inequality within and among countries**
Targets: 10.1 (income growth for bottom 40%), 10.2 (social/economic/political inclusion), 10.3 (equal opportunity), 10.4 (fiscal/wage/social protection policies), 10.5 (financial regulation), 10.6 (developing country representation in global governance), 10.7 (migration), 10.a (special/differential treatment for LDCs), 10.b (ODA), 10.c (remittance costs)
*WSIS links: C6 (enabling environment for ICT access reducing inequality), C10 (ethics and equal digital rights)*

**Goal 11 — Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable**
Targets: 11.1 (safe/affordable housing), 11.2 (sustainable transport), 11.3 (sustainable urbanisation), 11.4 (cultural/natural heritage), 11.5 (disaster reduction), 11.6 (per capita environmental impact), 11.7 (public spaces), 11.a (urban-rural links), 11.b (Sendai Framework implementation), 11.c (resilient buildings in LDCs)
*WSIS links: C7 E-Government, C7 E-Environment*

**Goal 12 — Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns**
Targets: 12.1 (10-year sustainable production framework), 12.2 (natural resources), 12.3 (food waste halving), 12.4 (chemical management), 12.5 (waste reduction), 12.6 (corporate sustainability), 12.7 (sustainable public procurement), 12.8 (sustainable development awareness), 12.a (developing country scientific/tech capacity), 12.b (tourism), 12.c (fossil fuel subsidy reform)
*WSIS links: C7 E-Environment (e-waste); C8 (sustainable cultural production)*

**Goal 13 — Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts**
Targets: 13.1 (resilience to climate hazards), 13.2 (integrate climate measures), 13.3 (education on climate), 13.a ($100 billion climate finance), 13.b (capacity for climate planning in LDCs)
*WSIS links: C7 E-Environment, C7 E-Science*

**Goal 14 — Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources**
Targets: 14.1 (marine pollution), 14.2 (marine ecosystems), 14.3 (ocean acidification), 14.4 (overfishing), 14.5 (coastal conservation), 14.6 (fisheries subsidies), 14.7 (SIDS economic benefits), 14.a (marine technology transfer), 14.b (small-scale fishers), 14.c (UNCLOS implementation)
*WSIS links: C7 E-Science (ocean data and satellite monitoring)*

**Goal 15 — Protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems**
Targets: 15.1 (terrestrial/freshwater ecosystems), 15.2 (forests), 15.3 (desertification), 15.4 (mountain ecosystems), 15.5 (biodiversity loss), 15.6 (genetic resources), 15.7 (poaching/trafficking), 15.8 (invasive species), 15.9 (ecosystem values in planning), 15.a (finance for biodiversity), 15.b (sustainable forest management), 15.c (wildlife crime)
*WSIS links: C7 E-Environment, C7 E-Science (remote sensing)*

**Goal 16 — Promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development**
Targets: 16.1 (violence reduction), 16.2 (child abuse), 16.3 (rule of law), 16.4 (illicit financial flows), 16.5 (corruption), 16.6 (transparent institutions), 16.7 (participatory decision-making), 16.8 (developing country representation), 16.9 (legal identity/birth registration), 16.10 (access to information and fundamental freedoms), 16.a (build institutions to prevent violence), 16.b (non-discriminatory laws)
*WSIS links: C1, C6, C9 (media freedom), C10 (ethics), C7 E-Government; SDG 16.10 is most directly linked*

**Goal 17 — Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the Global Partnership**
Finance: 17.1–17.5 | Technology: 17.6–17.8 | Capacity-building: 17.9 | Trade: 17.10–17.12 | Systemic issues: 17.13–17.17 | Data, monitoring and accountability: 17.18–17.19
Key ICT targets: **17.8** — "Fully operationalize the technology bank and science, technology and innovation capacity-building mechanism for least developed countries by 2017 and enhance the use of enabling technology, in particular information and communications technology"
*WSIS links: C11 (international cooperation) aligns directly with SDG 17 entire structure*

### Most WSIS-Linked SDGs

**SDG 9.c** explicitly calls for "significantly increased access to information and communications technology and strive to provide universal and affordable access to the Internet in least developed countries by 2020" — the closest direct SDG target to the WSIS digital divide agenda.

**SDG 4** (Quality Education) connects deeply to WSIS C4 (Capacity Building) and C7 (E-Learning) — education technology, digital literacy, and teacher training.

**SDG 16** (Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions) — specifically 16.10 (access to information and fundamental freedoms) — aligns with C1 (governance), C9 (media independence), and C10 (ethics).

**SDG 17** (Partnerships for the Goals) is the meta-goal most aligned with WSIS's multi-stakeholder implementation model and C11 (International and Regional Cooperation).

### WSIS-SDG Matrix

The **[[wsis-sdg-matrix]]** is the official cross-reference tool developed by UN Action Line facilitators ([[ungis]]) and launched at the WSIS Forum 2015. Key findings from the full target-level mapping:

- **C3 (Access to Information and Knowledge)** links to all 17 SDGs — the most broadly connected Action Line
- **C10 (Ethical Dimensions)** links to 13 SDGs (nearly all except purely environmental)
- **C4 (Capacity Building)** and **C7 (ICT Applications)** together cover almost all SDG targets
- **C2 (Infrastructure)** is foundational — links primarily to SDGs 1, 8, 9, 11 (with SDG 9.c as the direct match)
- **C9 (Media)** has the narrowest SDG mapping — only 5.b, 9.c, 12.8, 16.10
- **C11 (International Cooperation)** maps to SDGs 17.9, 17.16, 17.17 — the partnership targets

See [[wsis-sdg-matrix]] for full target-level mapping. See [[ungis-gdc-matrix]] for the extended 2024 version adding GDC as a third dimension.

The WSIS+20 outcome ([[unga-res-80-173]], para. 113) requires expanded and more detailed mapping including specific targets and indicators by 2027.

### SDGs and the GDC

The [[global-digital-compact]] (2024) is positioned as a **digital implementation mechanism** for the 2030 Agenda — supplementing and reinforcing the SDGs' technology dimension. The GDC explicitly links to SDG 17 (means of implementation) and SDG 9 (innovation and infrastructure).

The three-way relationship between WSIS, SDGs, and GDC is the defining framework challenge for digital cooperation: WSIS provides the implementation machinery, SDGs provide the development goals, and the GDC provides the digital governance architecture.

## Open Questions
- With 2030 deadline approaching, are SDG 9.c connectivity targets on track? An estimated 2.6 billion people remain offline.
- How will the High-Level Political Forum (HLPF, annual SDG review) integrate post-2025 WSIS and GDC reporting without duplication?
- The WSIS-SDG Matrix linkage of C3 to all 17 SDGs — is this substantively meaningful, or a political artefact of broad framing?
- Post-2030, if a new development framework succeeds the SDGs, how will WSIS Action Lines be realigned?
