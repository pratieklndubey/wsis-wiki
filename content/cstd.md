# Commission on Science and Technology for Development (CSTD)

## Overview
The Commission on Science and Technology for Development (CSTD) is the United Nations intergovernmental body responsible for examining science and technology questions and their implications for development. Within the WSIS ecosystem, the CSTD serves as the primary formal UN channel for reviewing progress on WSIS implementation and provides the annual policy bridge between the [[wsis-forum]] and the UNGA.

The CSTD is a subsidiary body of the UN Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC). Its secretariat is provided by [[unctad]] in Geneva. Since 2006, following the WSIS Tunis Agenda, the CSTD has played an especially significant role as the forum to which the [[internet-governance-forum]] and other WSIS bodies report annually, and to which the WSIS+20 follow-up has assigned key deliverables.

## Key Facts
- **Full name:** Commission on Science and Technology for Development
- **Type:** Subsidiary body of the UN Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)
- **Secretariat:** Provided by [[unctad]], Geneva
- **Sessions:** Annual (meets in Geneva, usually April–May)
- **WSIS function:** Annual review of WSIS implementation; receives IGF outcomes; focal point for WSIS follow-up reporting
- **WSIS+20 deliverables assigned:**
  - **CSTD 29th session (2026):** Receive joint WSIS-GDC implementation roadmap from UNGIS (A/RES/80/173, para. 122)
  - **CSTD 30th session (2027):** Receive Action Line implementation roadmaps from facilitators (para. 113); receive ITU/UNGIS financing task force recommendations (para. 67)
- **IGF reporting:** IGF Secretariat reports annually to CSTD on IGF outcomes (A/RES/80/173, para. 100)
- **Relationship to UNGA:** CSTD reports annually to ECOSOC; ECOSOC reports to UNGA

## Detail

### CSTD Mandate and Composition

The CSTD was established by UNGA resolution 46/235 (1992). It replaced the earlier Intergovernmental Committee on Science and Technology for Development. The Commission:

- Has **43 member states** elected by ECOSOC for four-year terms (rotating regional representation)
- Meets **once per year** for a one-week session in Geneva (usually May)
- Produces an **annual report to ECOSOC** covering major themes in science, technology, and innovation for development
- Organises **intersessional panels** and expert groups on specific thematic topics

### CSTD and WSIS

The CSTD's WSIS role was formalised in the Tunis Agenda (2005) and reinforced through successive UNGA resolutions:

**Pre-WSIS+10:** The CSTD reviewed annual WSIS implementation reports, received the annual WSIS stocktaking report, and held dedicated WSIS agenda items at each session.

**WSIS+10 (2014–2015):** The WSIS+10 High-Level Event (June 2014, Geneva) was convened under CSTD auspices. The CSTD prepared the WSIS+10 Statement on Implementation of WSIS Outcomes ([[wsis-plus10-statement]]) that fed into the WSIS+10 Review at the UNGA.

**WSIS+20 (2025):** The CSTD played a key preparatory role in the WSIS+20 Review, feeding into the [[unga-res-80-173]] outcome. The resolution assigned the CSTD as the receiving body for key post-2025 deliverables (see Key Facts above).

### CSTD and IGF

The [[internet-governance-forum]] reports to the CSTD as part of its annual reporting cycle. This relationship is specified in the IGF's mandate (Tunis Agenda para. 72) and reaffirmed in [[unga-res-80-173]] (para. 100). In practice, the IGF Chair's Summary and MAG Chair's report are submitted to each CSTD session as a standing agenda item.

This reporting relationship means the CSTD is the formal UN body through which the IGF (a non-decisional forum) connects to the intergovernmental process — an important design feature of the WSIS Internet governance architecture.

### WSIS+20 Assigned Deliverables

[[unga-res-80-173]] (December 2025) assigned the CSTD the following key roles in post-2025 WSIS implementation:

| Session | Year | Deliverable |
|---------|------|-------------|
| CSTD 29th | 2026 | Receive and consider joint WSIS-GDC implementation roadmap from [[ungis]] (para. 122) |
| CSTD 30th | 2027 | Receive Action Line facilitator implementation roadmaps linking C1–C11 to SDG targets and GDC commitments (para. 113) |
| CSTD 30th | 2027 | Receive [[itu]]/UNGIS financing task force recommendations on digital development finance gaps (para. 67) |
| Annual | 2026+ | Receive annual IGF outcome reports (para. 100) |

These deliverables make the CSTD the single most important institutional node in the post-2025 WSIS-to-UNGA accountability chain.

## Connections
- Subsidiary body of ECOSOC; secretariat hosted by [[unctad]]
- Primary formal channel for [[wsis-action-lines]] progress review within UN intergovernmental system
- [[internet-governance-forum]] reports annually to CSTD (Tunis Agenda para. 72; A/RES/80/173 para. 100)
- Designated recipient for post-2025 WSIS+20 deliverables by [[unga-res-80-173]] (paras. 67, 113, 122)
- Works with [[ungis]] on WSIS-GDC coherence (joint roadmap deliverable for CSTD 2026)
- Convened [[wsis-plus10-statement]] High-Level Event (2014)
- Feeds annual reports to ECOSOC → UNGA, making it the formal reporting chain for WSIS+20
