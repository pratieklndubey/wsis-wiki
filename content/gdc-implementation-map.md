# GDC Implementation Map

## Overview
The **GDC Implementation Map** is a document mandated by paragraph 71 of the [[global-digital-compact]] (GDC), directing the UN Secretary-General to provide a Compact implementation map for the consideration of governments and other stakeholders. The map reflects contributions of the UN system and other relevant stakeholders, and was required to be submitted to the Secretary-General's report on progress in WSIS implementation ahead of the WSIS+20 review.

The working draft (Version 31 July 2025) was produced by the **UN Working Group on Digital Technologies (WGDT)**, a body that coordinated the UN system response to the GDC. The map is built on the WSIS-2030 Agenda-GDC Matrix originally developed by [[ungis]], and demonstrates how existing UN mechanisms — most prominently the WSIS Action Lines and their facilitating agencies — already operationalise GDC commitments.

## Key Facts
- **Mandated by:** GDC paragraph 71 (GDC adopted 22–23 September 2024)
- **Version:** Draft, 31 July 2025
- **Produced by:** UN Working Group on Digital Technologies (WGDT)
- **Built on:** [[ungis]] WSIS-2030 Agenda-GDC Matrix
- **Purpose:** Map UN system activities against GDC commitments; demonstrate complementarity with WSIS and 2030 Agenda
- **Implementation phases:** 3-phase structure through 82nd GA session (2027 High-Level Review)
- **Funding mechanisms:** UN Development Account, Joint SDG Fund, Multi-Partner Trust Funds (MPTFs)
- **Primary tracking system:** EOSG Tracking System

## Detail

### Mandate and Genesis

GDC paragraph 71 reads: "To track and monitor progress, we request the Secretary-General to provide a Compact implementation map for the consideration of Governments and other stakeholders that reflects the contributions of the United Nations system and other relevant stakeholders and to reflect this in the report of the Secretary-General on progress made in the implementation of and follow-up to the outcomes of the World Summit on the Information Society at the regional and international levels ahead of the WSIS+20 review."

The implementation map thus serves dual purposes: (1) GDC tracking mechanism and (2) WSIS+20 review input. The WGDT was created to coordinate UN-wide response to the GDC and to produce this map.

### Structure: Three Implementation Phases

**Phase 1 — Foundation and Early Action (until September 2025)**
- Immediate steps: designating coordination points within UN entities
- Early implementation of GDC commitments with existing resources
- Internal UN system coordination (WGDT, UNGIS, ODET)
- Alignment of UN programmes and policies with GDC objectives

**Phase 2 — Scale Up (until September 2026)**
- Scale up to country and regional levels
- Deepen private sector engagement
- Strengthen multi-stakeholder platforms
- Reporting to CSTD 29th session (2026) via UNGIS joint WSIS-GDC roadmap

**Phase 3 — Consolidation (September 2026 onwards)**
- Consolidate gains from Phases 1 and 2
- Prepare for the 82nd GA High-Level Review of the GDC (2027)
- Full integration of GDC reporting with WSIS stocktaking

### Funding Architecture

The implementation map identifies three primary funding channels:

1. **UN Development Account** — Core UN system operational financing for inter-agency coordination and analytical work
2. **Joint SDG Fund** — Integrated financing mechanism enabling cross-pillar SDG-GDC programming; includes Digital Window
3. **Multi-Partner Trust Funds (MPTFs)** — Flexible pooled financing allowing private sector and philanthropic contributions; used for Digital Infrastructure Investment Initiative and related initiatives

### Tracking and Monitoring Platforms

The map identifies six primary monitoring platforms:

| Platform | Lead | Function |
|----------|------|----------|
| **EOSG Tracking System** | Executive Office of the SG | Central GDC implementation tracking |
| **WSIS Stocktaking Database** | [[itu]] | 19,000+ project entries mapped to Action Lines |
| **Partner2Connect** | [[itu]] | Digital connectivity pledge tracking |
| **Digital Cooperation Portal** (forthcoming) | ODET | Unified GDC-WSIS reporting portal |
| **ITU Data Hub** | [[itu]] | ICT connectivity and development statistics |
| **UNDP Digital Development Compass** | UNDP | Country-level digital development data |
| **UNESCO ROAM-X Indicators** | UNESCO | Internet universality measurement |

### Key Multistakeholder Platforms Recognised

The implementation map recognises the following as primary multistakeholder implementation mechanisms:
- **WSIS architecture** (WSIS Forum, Action Line Facilitation, WSIS Stocktaking, WSIS Prizes)
- **Technology Facilitation Mechanism (TFM)** — established by 2030 Agenda para. 70
- **OHCHR Business and Technology Project (B-Tech)** — human rights in digital contexts
- **STI Forum** (Science, Technology and Innovation for SDGs)
- **[[internet-governance-forum]] (IGF)** — including NRIs (National and Regional Initiatives)
- **[[wsis-forum]]** — annual platform
- **AI for Good Summit** — ITU-led AI governance platform

### Relationship to WSIS Action Lines

The implementation map is structured around the five GDC objectives, and for each GDC action point it identifies:
1. The relevant SDG(s)
2. The relevant [[wsis-action-lines]] (C1–C11 and sub-themes)
3. Existing UN mechanisms already implementing that commitment

This structure demonstrates that the overwhelming majority of GDC commitments already have institutional homes within the WSIS framework — making the case for complementarity rather than duplication.

### Office for Digital and Emerging Technologies (ODET)

The implementation map references the establishment of ODET (Office for Digital and Emerging Technologies) — mandated by [[unga-res-80-173]] para. 109 — as the new system-wide coordination office building on the Secretary-General's Envoy on Technology. ODET is expected to host the Digital Cooperation Portal (tracking platform) and coordinate GDC reporting to UNGA sessions.

## Connections
- Mandated by [[global-digital-compact]] para. 71
- Built on [[ungis-gdc-matrix]] (WSIS-2030 Agenda-GDC Matrix)
- All GDC objectives mapped to [[wsis-action-lines]] (C1–C11)
- Primary tracking through WSIS Stocktaking maintained by [[itu]]
- Three-phase structure feeds into [[cstd]] 29th session (2026) via [[ungis]] joint roadmap
- [[internet-governance-forum]] and [[wsis-forum]] recognised as primary implementation platforms
- ODET (established by [[unga-res-80-173]]) to host new Digital Cooperation Portal
- Aligned with WSIS+20 mandate to strengthen WSIS-GDC coherence
