# C1: The Role of Governments and All Stakeholders in the Promotion of ICTs for Development

## Overview
Action Line C1 is the foundational governance pillar of the WSIS framework, addressing the roles, responsibilities, and partnerships among all categories of stakeholders — governments, private sector, civil society, international organisations, and the technical community — in advancing ICT-for-development. It establishes the institutional and political logic underlying the entire WSIS Action Line system: that no single actor can drive the Information Society alone, and that structured cooperation across stakeholder groups is essential.

C1 was defined in Section C of the [[geneva-plan-of-action]] (12 December 2003) and assigned to ECOSOC and the UN Regional Commissions as facilitators, with [[itu]] as co-facilitator. Its central implementation tool is the **national e-strategy** — a government-led, multi-stakeholder plan integrating ICT into national development priorities. WSIS called on all governments to develop national e-strategies by 2005; uptake was uneven but the concept shaped national digital policy processes globally for two decades.

C1 is closely linked to [[c6]] (Enabling Environment), which addresses the regulatory and legal frameworks that make ICT governance functional, and to [[c11]] (International and Regional Cooperation), which addresses north-south and south-south dimensions of stakeholder cooperation.

## Detail

### Scope and Purpose

C1 addresses three dimensions of governance for ICT development:

**1. Stakeholder roles:** The Geneva Plan of Action defines distinct and complementary roles:
- *Governments:* Lead role in formulating national e-strategies; creating enabling legal and regulatory environments; building e-government services; mainstreaming ICT in development plans
- *Private sector:* Responsible for infrastructure investment, content creation, and applications; expected to contribute to sustainable development
- *Civil society:* Essential for achieving equitable outcomes; participates in designing ICT policies and initiatives; champions public interest dimensions
- *International organisations:* Integrate ICT into their development cooperation programmes; support national capacity

**2. National e-strategies:** WSIS called on all governments to develop national e-strategies by 2005, adapted to each country's needs. These strategies were to mainstream ICT across key development sectors — education, health, agriculture, governance — and to foster an enabling environment for private sector investment. By WSIS+10 (2015), the majority of developed countries and a growing share of developing countries had adopted some form of national digital strategy. By 2025, virtually all UN member states had national digital strategies or transformation roadmaps.

**3. Multi-sector partnerships:** C1 calls explicitly for public-private partnerships (PPPs) and for ICT showcases demonstrating best practices. It also calls for the engagement of indigenous peoples and communities in shaping the Information Society.

### Key Geneva Plan of Action Provisions

The C1 section of the [[geneva-plan-of-action]] instructs:
- Governments to integrate ICT targets in national development plans, PRSPs, and MDG implementation frameworks
- Governments to develop national e-strategies by 2005 (with international cooperation support)
- International organisations to mainstream ICT in their programmes
- Partnerships between governments, private sector, and civil society to be documented as WSIS Thematic Meetings and best practice exchanges
- ICT indicators and benchmarking frameworks to be developed (feeding into the Partnership on Measuring ICT for Development)

### SDG Connections

C1 connects to SDGs through the governance and partnership dimensions:
- **SDG 1** (No Poverty): ICT for poverty reduction; digital financial inclusion
- **SDG 3** (Good Health): Targets 3.8 (universal health coverage) and 3.d (health security) through ICT-enabled health systems
- **SDG 5** (Gender Equality): ICT as a tool for women's economic empowerment and political participation
- **SDG 10** (Reduced Inequalities): Target 10.c — remittance costs; ICT-enabled financial transfers
- **SDG 16** (Peace, Justice, Strong Institutions): Targets 16.5 (reduce corruption), 16.6 (accountable institutions), 16.10 (public access to information) — all enabled by e-government and transparency initiatives
- **SDG 17** (Partnerships): Target 17.18 — statistical capacity, data disaggregation, ICT indicators

### WSIS+20 Mandate (2025)

[[unga-res-80-173]] (December 2025) issued the following mandates relevant to C1:
- **Para. 18:** Reaffirmed all Action Lines, including C1, as "the main framework for translating the World Summit vision into concrete outcomes"
- **Para. 111:** C1 facilitators to ensure close alignment with the 2030 Agenda
- **Para. 112:** Human rights, gender equality, and non-discrimination must be core themes within C1 work; UNHCHR and UN-Women to be engaged
- **Para. 113:** C1 facilitators to develop an implementation roadmap linking C1 to specific SDG targets and GDC commitments, with targets, indicators, and metrics; report to CSTD 30th session (2027)
- **Para. 28:** Reaffirmed the [[multistakeholder-model]] as the basis for all WSIS implementation — the foundational principle of C1

### Key Themes

C1 facilitation meetings have consistently addressed: stakeholder cooperation and coordination; Millennium/Sustainable Development Goal mainstreaming; national digital strategy design and implementation; ICT for inclusive governance; open government data; anti-corruption through technology; indigenous peoples and digital rights; youth engagement in ICT governance; gender dimensions of multi-stakeholder governance.

## Connections
- Defined in [[geneva-plan-of-action]] Section C (2003); facilitators confirmed in [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]] Annex (2005)
- Institutionalises the [[multistakeholder-model]] as the operating principle for WSIS implementation
- Closely linked to [[c6]] (Enabling Environment) — C1 addresses who governs; C6 addresses how
- Linked to [[c11]] (International Cooperation) — C1's stakeholder roles have a north-south cooperation dimension
- Facilitator meetings held annually at [[wsis-forum]]; coordination through [[ungis]]
- Progress tracked in [[wsis-action-lines]] overview page and ITU WSIS Stocktaking database
- Roadmap due to [[cstd]] 2027

## Open Questions
- National e-strategies were called for by 2005 — to what extent have LDCs and small island states produced meaningful strategies?
- Has C1 kept pace with the shift from "ICT governance" to "digital governance" — are the facilitation mechanisms adequate for AI governance, platform regulation, and data sovereignty debates?
- The overlapping roles of C1 (governance roles) and C6 (enabling environment) have created some confusion — should these be merged or more clearly differentiated in future WSIS architecture?
- How will the required 2027 implementation roadmap define accountability for stakeholder role commitments?
