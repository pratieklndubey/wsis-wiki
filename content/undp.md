# United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)

## Overview
The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) is the UN's global development network, headquartered in New York City. Founded on **22 November 1965**, UNDP works in 177 countries to help eliminate poverty, reduce inequalities, and build resilient societies. It is "the world's largest provider of development grants and technical cooperation."

Within the WSIS ecosystem, UNDP serves as the **lead facilitator of Action Line C6** (Enabling Environment) and co-facilitates C4 (Capacity Building), C7 E-Government, and C11 (International and Regional Cooperation). UNDP is a co-organiser of the annual [[wsis-forum]] and a Vice-Chair of [[ungis]].

## Detail

### History and Structure

UNDP was created in 1965 by merging two predecessor bodies: the United Nations Expanded Programme of Technical Assistance (UNEPTA, 1949) and the UN Special Fund (1958). It operates under the authority of the UNGA and ECOSOC.

**Governance:**
- **Executive Board:** 36 members from 5 regional groups; provides intergovernmental support and supervision
- **Administrator:** Heads UNDP; appointed by UN Secretary-General, confirmed by UNGA
- **Associate Administrator** and four regional directors support the Administrator

**Programmes and initiatives:**
1. Democratic governance and peacebuilding
2. Poverty reduction and inclusive growth
3. Environment and climate change
4. Resilience and crisis response
5. HIV/AIDS and health

**Innovation Facility (2014):** Accelerates innovation in development; operates across five regions.

### UNDP's WSIS Role: C6 (Enabling Environment)

UNDP leads Action Line C6, which covers the legal, regulatory, and policy frameworks enabling a conducive environment for the Information Society. Sixteen facilitation meetings have been held since 2006. The C6 thematic scope includes:

- ICT regulatory and legal frameworks
- E-commerce legislation
- Intellectual property rights
- Internet governance policy
- Consumer protection online
- Competition policy for digital markets
- Domain name management
- Radio frequency spectrum governance

UNDP's development mandate shapes its approach to C6: enabling environments are framed primarily in terms of ensuring digital development benefits reach marginalized populations, not just creating business-friendly regulatory regimes. The co-facilitator arrangement — ITU, UN Regional Commissions, UNCTAD, UN DESA, UNIDO, and APC — reflects the breadth of C6's scope.

### UNDP and E-Government

UNDP co-facilitates C7 E-Government alongside ITU, with day-to-day coordination provided by UN DESA (Deniz Susar as lead). E-Government covers:
- Transparent and efficient public administration through ICT
- Digital public services
- Open government data
- E-participation and civic technology

UNDP's democratic governance programme makes it a natural lead for the e-government dimension.

### Human Development Report and ICT

UNDP's flagship publication, the **Human Development Report** (annual since 1991), periodically addresses ICT as a development dimension. The 2001 HDR (*Making New Technologies Work for Human Development*) was particularly influential in framing the digital divide as a human development challenge, contributing to the political environment that gave rise to WSIS.

### UNDP and SDGs

UNDP is the UN's primary operational SDG implementation body, with the most extensive country-level footprint in the UN system. UNDP administers the UNAIDS cosponsorship arrangement, coordinates Resident Coordinators in 130+ countries, and manages the largest share of UN development system resources.

ICT cuts across UNDP's entire SDG portfolio — from digital financial inclusion (SDG 1, 10) to e-government (SDG 16) to digital infrastructure (SDG 9).
