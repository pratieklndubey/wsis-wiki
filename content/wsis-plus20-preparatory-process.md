# WSIS+20 Preparatory Process (2022–2025)

## Overview
The WSIS+20 Preparatory Process was the multi-year programme of consultations, regional events, and high-level meetings that led to the UNGA Overall Review of WSIS implementation in December 2025. Mandated by UNGA Resolution A/RES/70/125 (2015) — the outcome of [[wsis-review-2015]] — the process engaged governments, UN agencies, civil society, the private sector, academia, and the technical community across three years of structured dialogue.

The preparatory process was coordinated by a core group of UN agencies — [[itu]], [[unesco]], [[undp]], [[unctad]], UN DESA, [[cstd]], and the five UN Regional Commissions — under the umbrella of [[ungis]]. It was distinguished from earlier WSIS review processes by its close integration with the [[global-digital-compact]] negotiations (adopted September 2024), requiring the preparatory process to simultaneously define WSIS's relationship to the GDC while making the case for WSIS continuity beyond 2025.

The process culminated in the adoption of [[unga-res-80-173]] (A/RES/80/173) on 17 December 2025 by the UN General Assembly, following a High-Level Meeting on 15–17 December 2025 in New York. The outcome made the [[internet-governance-forum]] a permanent UN forum, mandated WSIS+30 in 2035, and called for action line implementation roadmaps by 2027.

## Key Facts
- **Mandate:** A/RES/70/125 (December 2015), operative para. 8 — called for 2025 UNGA overall review
- **Duration:** Formal preparatory process 2022–2025 (consultation phase from 2023)
- **Co-facilitators (UNGA New York):** H.E. Ekitela Lokaale (Kenya) and H.E. Suela Janina (Albania)
- **Lead agencies:** [[itu]], [[unesco]], [[undp]], [[unctad]], UN DESA, [[cstd]], UN Regional Commissions
- **Culminating event:** UNGA High-Level Meeting, 15–17 December 2025, New York
- **Side events (Dec 2025):** "Celebrating Twenty Years" (ITU/UNDP/UNESCO/Switzerland); "UN in Action for Digital Cooperation" (UNGIS); "Digital@UNGA — WSIS+20 Edition"
- **Outcome:** [[unga-res-80-173]] — adopted 17 December 2025

## Detail

### Mandate and Institutional Context

UNGA Resolution A/RES/70/125 (adopted 16 December 2015) established the WSIS+20 mandate in its operative paragraph 8, calling for a "high-level meeting of the General Assembly in 2025 to conduct an overall review of the implementation of the WSIS outcomes." This 10-year review cycle mirrored the WSIS+10 process (2015) which itself reviewed the decade since Geneva 2003.

The WSIS+20 review was more complex than WSIS+10 because it coincided with:
1. Negotiations on the **Global Digital Compact (GDC)** — adopted at the UN Summit of the Future, 22–23 September 2024 — which covered overlapping thematic ground (connectivity, digital inclusion, AI governance, multistakeholder processes)
2. The **rapid evolution of AI and emerging technologies** — requiring the review to address topics not contemplated in the 2003–2005 WSIS documents
3. **Accelerating digital divides** — with 2.6 billion people still offline in 2024, despite significant progress since 2003

### Preparatory Timeline

#### 2022–2023: Early Consultations and Framework Setting

- **CSTD WSIS+20 Open Consultation (2022–2023):** The [[cstd]], as the primary UN intergovernmental body for WSIS follow-up, initiated stakeholder consultations to gather inputs on WSIS achievements, gaps, and priorities for the 2025 review.
- **SDG Summit, September 2023:** WSIS framed as a critical implementation mechanism for the 2030 Agenda; ministers of digital affairs participated in side events.
- **IGF 2023, Kyoto (October 2023):** The 18th IGF included a dedicated WSIS+20 High-Level Panel with UN officials and government ministers, and a WSIS High-Level Dialogue. Key messages: WSIS remains relevant; IGF institutional status must be resolved; AI governance requires multistakeholder approaches aligned with WSIS principles.
- **UN ESCAP Regional Preparatory Meeting, Yerevan (November 2023):** First of the regional commission preparatory events feeding inputs from the Asia-Pacific region into the WSIS+20 process.

#### 2024: The High-Level Preparatory Year

- **UN ECE Regional Preparatory Meeting, Geneva (March 2024):** European inputs; emphasis on digital sovereignty, cybersecurity standards, and AI regulation.
- **UN ESCWA Regional Preparatory Meeting, Dubai (May 2024):** Arab states perspectives on digital inclusion, Arabic content, and sovereign internet.
- **WSIS+20 Forum High-Level Event 2024, Geneva (27–31 May 2024):**
  - 4,000+ participants from 160 countries; ~80 ministers, deputies, and regulatory heads
  - Chaired by H.E. Albert Rösti (Federal Councillor, Switzerland)
  - Substantive agenda: WSIS+20 review, GDC/Summit of the Future, digital public infrastructure (DPI), AI governance, virtual reality, quantum technologies, generative AI, cybersecurity, digital trust
  - Outputs: Chair's Summary; Outcome Document; High-Level Outcomes and Executive Brief
  - Central message: "WSIS is an existing and proven framework for implementing global digital commitments — avoid duplication with GDC"; calls for GDC to build on WSIS architecture
- **UN Summit of the Future, New York (22–23 September 2024):** Adopted the Global Digital Compact and the Pact for the Future. The GDC's five objectives (universal connectivity; digital inclusion; AI governance; digital public goods; data governance) closely mirrored WSIS themes, creating both opportunity for synergy and risk of duplication. [[ungis]] developed the UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix as a mapping tool.

#### 2025: Final Preparatory Phase

- **UN ESCWA Regional Preparatory Meeting, Amman (February 2025):** Final Arab region inputs.
- **CSTD 28th Session (April 2025):** The CSTD adopted its 20-year WSIS review report — a key input document for the UNGA negotiations.
- **UNESCO Consultations (June 2025):** Thematic consultations on cultural diversity, education, media freedom, and ethical dimensions of AI in the context of WSIS Action Lines C8–C10.
- **IGF 2025, Oslo (June 2025):** The 20th IGF — a milestone year — carried a strong WSIS+20 theme; discussions on IGF's permanent status and future institutional arrangements directly fed into UNGA resolution negotiations.
- **WSIS+20 High-Level Event 2025, Geneva (7–11 July 2025):**
  - 11,000+ participants from 169 countries; 59 ministers; 56 regulatory heads; 60+ UN representatives
  - Co-located with the AI for Good Global Summit — underscoring the WSIS-AI nexus
  - Chaired by H.E. Solly Malatsi (Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies, South Africa)
  - Keynote by H.E. Alar Karis (President, Republic of Estonia)
  - Key themes: AI governance; generative AI; digital public infrastructure; cybersecurity; 5G and next-generation connectivity; space technologies; smart sustainable cities; digital public goods; youth and gender
  - 19 WSIS Prizes 2025 winners recognised; 71 champions; 270 nominees
  - WSIS+20 Ministerial Roundtable: 59 ministers discussed national digital priorities; consensus around avoiding GDC/WSIS duplication and strengthening WSIS architecture
  - Chair's Summary called for: enhanced progress-tracking; clear targets under WSIS Action Lines; WSIS as the central multistakeholder platform grounded in human rights and sustainability

### UNGA Negotiation Phase (September–December 2025)

Following the July 2025 high-level events, the UNGA First Committee and Second Committee in New York undertook negotiations on the WSIS+20 outcome text, co-facilitated by Kenya and Albania. Key negotiating tensions:
- **IGF permanent status:** Whether to make IGF permanent (ultimately achieved in para. 99)
- **GDC relationship:** Ensuring WSIS and GDC are complementary, not duplicative (paras. 106, 120, 122)
- **Internet shutdowns:** Language on states refraining from internet shutdowns (para. 76)
- **AI governance:** Whether the WSIS outcome or the GDC should be primary on AI (GDC was affirmed as primary; WSIS noted)
- **Connectivity targets:** Whether to set new quantitative targets (no new binding targets were set)

The High-Level Meeting was held on **15 December 2025**, with the resolution adopted on **17 December 2025** as [[unga-res-80-173]].

### Six Thematic Focus Areas

The preparatory process organised WSIS+20 work around six thematic pillars, derived from stakeholder consultations:

1. **Universal and meaningful connectivity** — Assessing progress; 68% of world online in 2024 but 2.6 billion still offline
2. **Capacity building and digital skills** — Digital literacy; AI skills; gender equity in training
3. **Cybersecurity** — Trust in digital systems; child online protection; national cybersecurity strategies; CIRTs
4. **Emerging technologies** — AI, IoT, 5G, quantum computing; implications for development and governance
5. **Digital cooperation** — Multistakeholder partnerships; WSIS–GDC relationship; coordination architecture
6. **Sustainable use of technology** — E-waste; energy consumption from data centres; green ICT; climate action

### Key Inputs to the Process

- **ITU Secretary-General's Report on WSIS+20** (published pre-review): Comprehensive review of 20 years of implementation; baseline comparisons for all Geneva Plan of Action targets; case for WSIS continuity
- **UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix** (2024): Mapping of all 5 GDC objectives against WSIS Action Lines and SDGs; produced by 28 UN agencies to demonstrate complementarity
- **WSIS Stocktaking data:** 19,000+ entries; WSIS Prizes; 50,000+ annual forum participants — evidence of ongoing engagement
- **Civil society and private sector submissions:** Hundreds of inputs through CSTD and ITU online consultation portals
- **Regional commission reports:** Five regional inputs from Africa, Asia-Pacific, Europe, Arab States, and Latin America

## Connections
- Mandated by [[wsis-review-2015]] (A/RES/70/125, para. 8)
- Coordinated by [[itu]], [[unesco]], [[undp]], [[unctad]], [[ungis]], [[cstd]]
- Annual [[wsis-forum]] served as principal stakeholder preparatory platform (2023–2025)
- Closely linked to [[global-digital-compact]] negotiations (September 2024)
- [[internet-governance-forum]] 2023 and 2025 served as key preparatory venues
- Outcome: [[unga-res-80-173]] (A/RES/80/173, 17 December 2025)
- Led to WSIS+30 mandate for 2035 review
- Resulted in implementation roadmap mandate for all [[wsis-action-lines]] by CSTD 2027

## Open Questions
- Were civil society and technical community inputs weighted differently from government positions in shaping the final outcome text?
- The co-facilitator pairing (Kenya–Albania) was designed to balance north-south perspectives — to what extent did this influence key negotiating outcomes such as the internet shutdowns language?
- The preparatory process did not produce an agreed definition of how WSIS and the GDC should relate institutionally — the WSIS-GDC joint roadmap (due to CSTD 2026) must resolve this in practice
- Was the WSIS+20 preparatory process more or less inclusive than WSIS+10 in terms of civil society and private sector participation?
- Did the co-location of the 2025 HLE with the AI for Good Summit help or hinder the preparatory process focus?
