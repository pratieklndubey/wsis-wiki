# WSIS Wiki Index
Last updated: 2026-04-08
Total pages: 48

## Process Pages
- [[wsis-phase-i-geneva-2003]] — First phase of the World Summit on the Information Society, Geneva, 10–12 December 2003; produced the Geneva Declaration of Principles and Geneva Plan of Action
- [[wsis-phase-ii-tunis-2005]] — Second and final phase of WSIS, Tunis, 16–18 November 2005; produced the Tunis Commitment and Tunis Agenda, and created the Internet Governance Forum
- [[wsis-review-2015]] — WSIS+10 Review; UNGA high-level meeting, December 2015; adopted Resolution A/RES/70/125 aligning WSIS with SDGs and calling for WSIS+20
- [[wsis-review-2025]] — WSIS+20 Review; UNGA overall review held 15–17 December 2025; adopted A/RES/80/173; IGF made permanent; WSIS+30 in 2035
- [[wsis-plus20-preparatory-process]] — Multi-year preparatory process 2022–2025 for the WSIS+20 Review; key milestones from regional consultations to July 2025 HLE; co-facilitated by ITU, UNESCO, UNDP, UNCTAD, UN DESA, CSTD
- [[wsis-stocktaking]] — WSIS Stocktaking Database maintained by ITU since 2004; 19,000+ entries; 2M+ users; WSIS Prizes (18 categories, 252 winners); mandated by Tunis Agenda and reaffirmed by WSIS+20
- [[wgig]] — Working Group on Internet Governance (2004–2005); 40 members; produced the landmark internet governance definition and four oversight models; led to creation of the IGF

## Document Pages
- [[geneva-declaration-of-principles]] — Core vision document from WSIS Phase I; establishes 11 key principles for an inclusive, people-centred Information Society; adopted 12 December 2003
- [[geneva-plan-of-action]] — Operational companion to the Geneva Declaration; defines 11 Action Lines (C1–C11), connectivity targets for 2015, and the Digital Solidarity Agenda; adopted 12 December 2003
- [[tunis-commitment]] — Political reaffirmation of Geneva principles at WSIS Phase II; extends commitments on gender, ICT for peace, and digital divide financing; adopted 18 November 2005
- [[tunis-agenda-for-the-information-society]] — Operational document from WSIS Phase II; created the Internet Governance Forum (IGF), assigned UN agency Action Line facilitators, and established WSIS implementation machinery; adopted 18 November 2005
- [[wsis-plus10-statement]] — WSIS+10 Statement on the Implementation of WSIS Outcomes (2014); one of two outputs from WSIS+10 High-Level Event; 22 progress points, 30 challenges including new themes (e-waste, media literacy, privacy); reaffirmed Geneva Plan of Action as still valid
- [[wsis-plus10-vision]] — WSIS+10 Vision for WSIS Beyond 2015 (2014); companion to the WSIS+10 Statement; forward-looking document covering Internet governance, IGF mandate, enhanced cooperation, and post-2015 development framing; fed into UNGA A/RES/70/125
- [[unga-res-80-173]] — WSIS+20 Outcome Resolution (A/RES/80/173, 17 December 2025); 127 paragraphs; landmark decisions: IGF made permanent UN forum; WSIS+30 review in 2035; action line facilitator roadmaps by 2027; WSIS-GDC coherence framework
- [[wsis-sdg-matrix]] — WSIS-SDG Matrix; launched WSIS Forum 2015; produced by 12 UN Action Line facilitators; maps C1–C11 (with C7 sub-themes) to specific SDG targets at target level; C3 links to all 17 SDGs; Annexes include rationale and ICT indicators
- [[ungis-gdc-matrix]] — WSIS Process and 2030 Agenda–GDC Matrix (UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix); produced 2024–2025 by 28 UN agencies; 71-page 4-column matrix mapping all 5 GDC objectives × WSIS Action Lines × SDGs × existing UN mechanisms; authoritative complementarity tool
- [[gdc-implementation-map]] — GDC Implementation Map (Draft, 31 July 2025); mandated by GDC para. 71; produced by UN WGDT; 3-phase implementation timeline; identifies primary tracking platforms (WSIS Stocktaking, Partner2Connect, ITU Data Hub, ROAM-X) and recognises WSIS architecture as primary delivery vehicle

## Institution Pages
- [[itu]] — International Telecommunication Union; lead UN agency for both WSIS phases; oldest UN specialised agency (founded 1865); headquarters Geneva; hosts annual WSIS Forum; maintains WSIS Stocktaking database (19,000+ entries); co-leads AI for Good, Broadband Commission, Giga, Partner2Connect; current SG: Doreen Bogdan-Martin
- [[internet-governance-forum]] — Created by Tunis Agenda para. 72 (2005); non-binding, non-decisional annual multistakeholder forum; met annually since Athens 2006; made permanent UN forum by A/RES/80/173 (2025); includes full meeting history (2006–2025), key themes by era, MAG structure, NRI ecosystem (170+), relationships to WSIS/UNGIS/CSTD/GDC
- [[icann]] — Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers; founded 1998; manages DNS and IP address allocation; central WSIS controversy 2003–2005; 2016 IANA transition from US government oversight; multistakeholder governance model
- [[isoc]] — Internet Society; founded 1992; technical community voice in WSIS; parent of IETF; advocates for open, globally connected internet; present at all major WSIS and IGF events
- [[rirs]] — Five Regional Internet Registries (AFRINIC, APNIC, ARIN, LACNIC, RIPE NCC); manage IP address allocation by region; technical community stakeholders in WSIS process; NRO represents them collectively
- [[unesco]] — UN Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization; founded 1945; Paris; 194 member states; lead/sole facilitator of WSIS Action Lines C3, C8, C9, C10; co-organiser of WSIS Forum; co-chairs AI ethics agenda (IAWG-AI) with ITU
- [[undp]] — UN Development Programme; founded 1965; New York; 177-country presence; lead facilitator of WSIS Action Line C6 (Enabling Environment); co-facilitates C4, C7 E-Government, C11; co-organiser of WSIS Forum
- [[unctad]] — UN Conference on Trade and Development; founded 1964; Geneva; current UNGIS Chair; provides CSTD secretariat; co-facilitates C4, C6, C7 E-Business; publishes Digital Economy Report; co-organiser of WSIS Forum
- [[unep]] — UN Environment Programme; founded 1972; Nairobi; co-facilitates C7 E-Environment; member of UNGIS; links WSIS to environmental SDGs (12–15)
- [[ungis]] — UN Group on the Information Society for Digital Transformation; established April 2006; 32-member inter-agency coordination body; ITU as permanent secretariat; currently chaired by UNCTAD; produces UNGIS-WSIS-GDC Matrix; mandated to deliver joint WSIS-GDC roadmap to CSTD 2026
- [[cstd]] — Commission on Science and Technology for Development; ECOSOC subsidiary body; UNCTAD secretariat; primary UN intergovernmental body for WSIS follow-up; IGF reports annually; key deliverable recipient in WSIS+20 (2026, 2027)

## Stakeholder Pages
- [[multistakeholder-model]] — The governance framework institutionalised by WSIS; four actor groups (governments, private sector, civil society, technical community); contrast with multilateral model; ongoing debates; embedded in all WSIS Action Lines

## Event Pages
- [[wsis-forum]] — Annual WSIS Forum hosted by ITU since 2009; world's largest ICT-for-development gathering (50,000+ participants); co-organised with UNESCO, UNDP, UNCTAD; includes WSIS Prizes (launched 2012)

## Issue Pages
- [[wsis-action-lines]] — Comprehensive overview of WSIS Action Lines C1–C11; includes descriptions, current facilitating agencies and named contacts, SDG connections, WSIS+20 roadmap mandate, and facilitation meeting counts; C7 has 8 sub-themes
- [[c1]] — C1: The Role of Governments and All Stakeholders in the Promotion of ICTs for Development; facilitator: ECOSOC/UN Regional Commissions/ITU; SDGs: 1, 3, 5, 10, 16, 17
- [[c2]] — C2: Information and Communication Infrastructure; facilitator: ITU; 16 meetings (2006–2021); SDGs: 1, 8, 9, 11; primary connectivity Action Line
- [[c3]] — C3: Access to Information and Knowledge; facilitators: ITU/UNESCO; 14 meetings; SDGs: all 17 — unique among Action Lines; covers open source, digital libraries, scientific open access
- [[c4]] — C4: Capacity Building; facilitators: UNDP/UNESCO/ITU/UNCTAD; 16 meetings; SDGs: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 12, 13, 14, 16, 17; covers digital skills, teacher training, R&D
- [[c5]] — C5: Building Confidence and Security in the Use of ICTs; facilitator: ITU; 16 meetings; SDGs: 1, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 11, 16, 17; covers cybersecurity, privacy, spam, child online protection
- [[c6]] — C6: Enabling Environment; facilitators: ITU/UNDP/UN Regional Commissions/UNCTAD; 16 meetings; SDGs: 2, 4, 5, 8, 9, 10, 11, 16, 17; mandated the WGIG; covers regulation, IP, spectrum, internet governance
- [[c7]] — C7: ICT Applications: Benefits in All Aspects of Life; 8 sub-themes (E-Government, E-Business, E-Learning, E-Health, E-Employment, E-Environment, E-Agriculture, E-Science); 15+ UN agencies; broadest coverage
- [[c8]] — C8: Cultural Diversity and Identity, Linguistic Diversity and Local Content; facilitator: UNESCO; 11 meetings; SDGs: 2, 4, 6, 8, 11, 12; covers multilingualism, heritage digitisation, local content, indigenous peoples
- [[c9]] — C9: Media; facilitator: UNESCO; 15 meetings; SDGs: 5, 9, 12, 16; covers press freedom, media pluralism, journalism safety, media literacy, disinformation
- [[c10]] — C10: Ethical Dimensions of the Information Society; facilitators: UNESCO/ECOSOC; 12 meetings; SDGs: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 16, 17; covers AI ethics, human rights, prevention of abusive uses
- [[c11]] — C11: International and Regional Cooperation; facilitators: UN Regional Commissions/UNDP/ITU/UNESCO/ECOSOC; SDG: 17 (targets 17.9, 17.16, 17.17); covers ODA mainstreaming, south-south cooperation, regional integration
- [[digital-divide]] — The gap between those who have access to ICTs and those who do not; WSIS central concern; dimensions: geographic, economic, gender, generational; 2.6 billion offline in 2024 despite progress since 2003
- [[internet-governance]] — The development and application of principles, norms, rules, decision-making and procedures that shape the evolution of the internet; WGIG definition; Tunis Agenda para. 34; four layers; WSIS contribution to the concept
- [[digital-solidarity-fund]] — Voluntary financing mechanism proposed in Geneva Plan of Action 2003; welcomed in Tunis Agenda 2005; championed the "Geneva Principle" (1% ICT procurement contribution); wound down; succeeded conceptually by Partner2Connect

## Connection Pages
- [[global-digital-compact]] — Adopted at UN Summit of the Future, September 2024; reinforces WSIS principles on connectivity, digital inclusion, and multistakeholder collaboration; adds AI governance dimension; WSIS-GDC joint implementation roadmap mandated for 2026
- [[sdgs]] — 17 Sustainable Development Goals; adopted by all UN member states September 2015; WSIS is positioned as implementation mechanism for digital dimensions of the SDGs; WSIS-SDG Matrix maps all 11 Action Lines to SDG targets; SDG 9.c most directly linked to WSIS connectivity agenda
- [[millennium-development-goals]] — 8 MDGs adopted at UN Millennium Summit 2000; MDG 8.F explicitly targeted ICT access; WSIS Geneva documents explicitly designed to advance MDG achievement; preceded and shaped the later WSIS-SDG alignment
